## The five convergences (what multiple dossiers say independently)

1. **Estrangement, not elapsed time, is the active ingredient of "cooling."**
   A (King's alien-relic test + the manufactured hacks: print/font/aloud/TTS),
   B (the peg clock kills cooling; same-day substitutes are re-presentation
   tricks), C (the predictability mechanism: the author is the maximally
   predicting reader of their own text; the one direct study found author
   blindness SURVIVED a two-week delay). The rest-gap is craft consensus;
   re-presentation is the mechanism a tool can actually serve.
2. **Phases are real but must be writer-performed, never tool-inferred.**
   C (recursion at the minutes scale is the field's strongest result; phases
   defensible only as attractor states), A (the same surface behavior means
   opposite things in different schools — cycling vs revision), E (don't model
   the lifecycle; derive it from what the writer did; expertise reversal:
   scaffolds that rescue novices tax experts).
3. **No school lets evaluation touch an incomplete draft.** A (the closed door
   extends to the project level — even human feedback waits for a complete
   draft), B (blurt first, revise after), C (what's actually supported is that
   premature SURFACE polish taxes generation — Glynn 1982, Kellogg WM; total
   evaluation lockout is stance, not science).
4. **The deadline is the engine; displays must bend, never shame.** D (behavior
   anchors to deadlines r=.82, plans r=.23; broken streaks leave you below
   baseline; repairable/task-framed lapse surfaces are the strongest cross-tier
   triangulation), E (recalculating-forward targets = the only deadline design
   with warm user sentiment; deficit displays = the best-documented
   demoralizer), A (contest deadlines are the most-cited productivity
   instrument in the culture).
5. **Pre-prose and post-prose material live inside the one document, by folk
   practice.** A (composting, idea files), B (notes accumulate at the bottom of
   the file), E (the graveyard/manuscraps pattern — dignify it rather than
   replace it with a binder).

# Dossier A — The short-story-in-two-weeks arc (craft literature + practitioner accounts)

[Verbatim-abridged from the fiction agent, 2026-07-03. Load-bearing quotes verified; folklore flagged.]

## Phase timeline (consensus arc, 2-week contest)
- **P0 Composting** (pre-window, ambient): ideas live OUTSIDE the manuscript — Bradbury noun lists, Le Guin composting (metaphor credited to Gary Snyder), idea files, Chiang's stickiness filter ("an idea that sticks for months tells me there's something compelling"). A contest prompt doesn't replace the reservoir; it QUERIES it.
- **P1 Ignition** (days 1-3): near-universal contest move = DISCARD THE FIRST IDEA (everyone has it; brainstorm ten). Documented NYCM participant burned days 1-4 on rejected ideas — normal. Mind: excitement + slot-anxiety. **Transition signal: THE ENDING EXISTS** (Chiang: "I don't begin drafting until I know the ending"; Kowal's pre-draft sequence ends with choosing the ending; Moorcock: no revelation not already established). Stories that stall are missing endings (Link's 18-month page-seven stall).
- **P2 Drafting burst** (days 3-8, but the TYPING IS HOURS): Kowal's Hugo-nominated story in 90 minutes; Ellison ~5h in bookstore windows; Jones budgets "two weeks to draft" inside a six-week cycle. Calendar time = gestation + revision, not typing. Mind: Lamott's "x-ray apron" despair / radio KFKD; occasionally VanderMeer's ecstatic possession. Doctrine: door closed, nobody sees it, short assignments. Transition: draft COMPLETE (Heinlein Rule 2 honored by every school).
- **P3 Cooling** (days 8-10; most deadline-distorted): **King's six weeks is BOOK-scale (verified) — porting it to shorts is the most common folklore error.** Short fiction: overnight-to-two-weeks (Gaiman "a few days," read printouts "as if you've never seen it before"; Weekend Warrior compresses to hours). **Transition signal: ESTRANGEMENT, not elapsed time** — King's "alien relic" test; Stafford's typo research gives the mechanism (you read your intention, not your page) and the hacks: print, change font, read aloud, TTS, different device. Estrangement can be MANUFACTURED.
- **P4 Revision passes** (days 10-13): macro-to-micro codified (HOCs/LOCs; Bell's three-draft system; rationale "don't polish sentences you'll cut"). Lamott: down draft / up draft / dental draft. Shorts: 2-4 passes. CUT-TO-FIT is its own sub-phase (King's 2nd = 1st − 10%; NYCM "counted words and shaved" to 2495/2500). Feedback enters HERE OR NEVER (King's Ideal Reader gets the rested second draft; Gaiman: readers "almost always right" about what's wrong, "almost always wrong" about the fix). Mind: cold-bloodedness replacing attachment (Bradbury: "This afternoon, burn down the house. Tomorrow, pour critical water upon the simmering coals").
- **P5 Finish + submit** (days 13-14): done-detection subjective and known to be — Saunders' P/N needle ("reread as a first-time reader… stop when the needle stays in P for the whole length"); Le Guin: completion judgment "can come only from the writer"; Valéry's abandonment (genuine, 1933). Last mile = LOGISTICS: Shunn standard manuscript format, blind-judging anonymization (name on MS = disqualification risk), portal friction, deadline spikes; disciplined pattern = submit early (19h before).

## Key findings
- Endings decided before drafting, across incompatible schools.
- Drafting is hours-to-days; the fortnight is before/after the typing.
- Estrangement is the actual transition mechanism and is synthesizable (font/print/aloud/device/TTS).
- EVERY school phase-separates generation from evaluation — even the anti-revision one (DWS cycling stays "in creative voice"; damns only COLD rewriting).
- First idea discarded on purpose under theme prompts.
- Read-aloud = the one revision practice with near-universal endorsement (Le Guin's mind's ear; Sedaris' audience-as-editor).
- No feedback until a complete draft exists (converging practice; only analogical science — Gollwitzer premature-completeness, flag: goals research).
- The deadline is contest culture's most-cited productivity instrument (WotF quarterly deadlines drive completion).
- Rejected entries have long afterlives ("Paper Menagerie" rejected → triple crown; "done but not finished" files).
- FOLKLORE FLAGS: Woolf "wet brush" UNATTRIBUTABLE (VW Society misquotations page) — do not use [NB: June core-loop dossier cites it — correct]. "Different, not better" untraceable. Bradbury "throw up in your typewriter" contested.

## Rival schools (honestly weighted)
- One-pass/cycling: Heinlein Rule 3 verbatim ("refrain from rewriting, except to editorial order"); DWS cycling (drift back every 400-600 words IN creative voice — revision never exists as a phase; "rewriting destroys… or dumbs it down"). Small, commercially real minority (high-volume indie genre); critics say it requires decades of internalized craft.
- Bradbury calibrated: "don't think"/story-a-week real, but he cut 50k from Something Wicked — speed was drafting-only; revision cold-blooded and real.
- Formula-outliners (Dent's Master Plot "no yarn written to the formula has yet failed to sell"; Budrys 7-point, still WotF courseware): NOT anti-revision — they RELOCATE the multi-pass work before the draft.
- No survey data on school proportions exists. Multiple VIABLE processes; multi-pass is not the only professional path.
- Link's when-stuck restart-from-page-one loop = literary-workshop cycling; the technique crosses school lines. **Same surface behavior means opposite things in different schools.**

## Pain points
Idea→commitment gap eats the window (nothing marks viability except the ending appearing) · estrangement under deadline is manual and scattered (a pile of workarounds for one need, none in the tool) · feedback logistics don't fit contest windows (Critters ~3wk queue) · cutting-to-fit is a distinct mode generic editors don't understand · doneness has no external criterion (premature submission ↔ infinite polish oscillation) · last 48h = logistics colliding with exhaustion.

## Strop implications
- FORBIDS a hard-coded multi-draft pipeline; phases must be writer-DECLARED, not inferred or enforced (same behavior ≠ same meaning across schools).
- THE CLOSED DOOR EXTENDS TO THE PROJECT LEVEL: no school lets evaluation touch an incomplete draft — even human feedback waits. AI diagnosis should default to silence until the writer declares the draft complete.
- Estrangement is the load-bearing transition and the one tools could actually serve: "make it read like someone else wrote it" as goal state; days-not-weeks calibration for shorts.
- Pre-draft material is a first-class artifact that is NOT the manuscript; the ending-known signal is the strongest documented predictor of a finishing draft.
- Macro-to-micro validates the three altitudes at project timescale; cut-to-target is a distinct evidence-backed late mode (structural cuts before line trims).
- Doneness belongs to the writer alone; the tool may surface the writer's own reactions (Saunders' needle IS self-diagnosis) but never render a completion verdict.
- Project arcs are NOT linear: dormancy, revival, re-targeting needed — not a one-way pipeline ending at "submitted."
- The finish is administrative (Shunn/anonymize/cap/portals) — where a calm tool either helps or becomes one more portal.

# Dossier B — The opinion-column arc (expert op-ed, ~800-1500 words)

[Verbatim-abridged from the column agent, 2026-07-03. Scenario: technologist op-ed on a court ruling's chilling effects.]

## Phase timeline
- **P0 The reservoir** (weeks-years, ambient): expert lives in the domain; material diffuse (head, notes files, tabs, prior posts). Klein: "By the time I am writing, I've done a lot of work." Exit: THE PEG FIRES (ruling drops).
- **P1 Ignition + angle test** (minutes-hours): peg decay clock (24-48h to submit; Volokh: "even a few days' delay can make your piece unsellable"). Entry condition = "a way in" (PG) / "Do I have a clear point? Who cares?" (HKS). Ruthless scope-narrowing: "Make a single point" (Duke). Exit: one-sentence claim exists.
- **P2 Evidence sweep** (2-6h, interleaved): opinion PDF, docket, case names/dates/holdings; evidence must end LINK-SHAPED (outlets require hyperlinks for fact-check). Lives in tabs/notes piles; PG: "accumulate notes at the bottom of the file". Canonical breakdown: "a ton of notes… and a blank screen" (Gerritt).
- **P3 The blurt** (one sitting, 1-5h practiced): fast bad draft both schools agree. PG "bad version 1 as fast as you can… loose, then tight"; McPhee "blurt out… anything". Exit: end-to-end draft, oversized, weak lede.
- **P4 Revision for argument** (≥ draft time; same-day + overnight): ordered passes — (a) DISTILL (compose the headline: what is this about?), (b) LEDE rebuilt against the now-known point, (c) STEELMAN ("to be sure" ¶ — a placed structural slot, second-to-last, editors select for it), (d) CUT 30-50% (Gerritt 30%; Zinsser 50%; hard caps 650-1200), (e) VERIFY (quotes/names/dates; author owns accuracy, desk re-checks), (f) READ ALOUD. Peer circulation substitutes for cooling (Fahs's 15 experts). Exit: fits cap while peg alive — DEADLINE-done, not aesthetically done.
- **P5 Submission limbo** (3d-weeks, out of hands): one outlet at a time, exclusivity, silence-as-rejection 3-7d.
- **P6 The edit burst** (24-72h, in the EDITOR'S medium): two intense edit rounds in 24h (Fahs); desk fact-check; headline belongs to the editor; happens in email/track-changes OUTSIDE the writer's tool; rewrite-of-rewrite flattens voice (Krugman: "flat and colorless").

## Key findings
- Op-ed formula = named teachable skeleton (hook → thesis early → 3 evidenced points → to-be-sure → kicker circling to lede); structure precedes words far more than fiction.
- Thesis-first is the PUBLISHED FORM; discovery still happens inside the draft (PG: 80% of ideas arrive after starting; 50% of starting ideas wrong). The two schools coexist in one process: distill-the-point-AFTER-draft-one is orthodox practice too (Trainor).
- TIME SIGNATURE INVERTS vs long-form: revision dominates (PG rereads drafts "50 times"); durations compress ~100x vs fiction.
- Cutting quantified: 30-50% of draft dies. What dies first: throat-clearing, background, credentials, HEDGES (Hall: academics hedge; PG: calibrate — "state baldly what you're sure of; hold dubious points at arm's length with perhapses").
- Whole branches amputated; sunk cost the named enemy ("keep something that isn't right because it cost you effort" = the most dangerous temptation).
- Peg clock KILLS cooling; substitutes = same-day distance tricks: read-aloud, persona-shift ("pretend to be a neutral reader"), rapid peer reads. [Convergence with C's predictability mechanism.]
- Fact-check split: author carries legal-technical accuracy; assertions can SEMANTICALLY DRIFT during cutting.
- Endgame adversarial + external: headline ownership, negotiated assertions, foreign edits returning as pasted text.
- Experts resent the form → defect to blogs/newsletters (Volokh).

## Fiction/column divergence (abridged)
Drafting discovers: story/voice (product) VS argument's shape (scaffolding, then hidden behind thesis-first surface) · Structure: emerges over drafts VS prefabricated slots · Time: first-draft-dominant, months VS revision-dominant, hours-days · Cooling: drawer-weeks VS killed-by-peg, replaced by re-presentation · Revision optimizes: voice preservation VS clarity/persuasion/cap-survival · Developmental = plot/POV/scenes VS unity/thesis-placement/evidence-order/steelman/kicker-echo · Evidence: none VS link-shaped, author-liable · Doneness: aesthetic VS deadline-relative · Editor: late holistic VS interventionist 24-72h loop · Overwork failure: prose loses life VS flat-and-colorless/hedged-to-mush.

## Pain points
Evidence fragmentation at the draft boundary (tabs→inline links, manual+lossy) · cutting under sunk cost, no protocol for "which ¶ is load-bearing" · fact-check has no checkpoint; claims drift during cuts · steelman easy to skip under deadline · cooling substitutes cost the missing hours · endgame breaks provenance (writer diffs foreign edits by eye in 24h) · qualification whiplash (over-hedge vs state-baldly; no pass reviews claim-strength as such).

## Strop implications
- Arc model must include a PRE-PROSE phase whose artifacts are links/quotes/one-sentence claim.
- Phase durations compress ~100x; never assume fiction rhythms (no "come back tomorrow" gates).
- Developmental altitude is FORM-AWARE: for argument = unity, thesis placement, evidence order, steelman presence, kicker-echo — or diagnostics read as noise.
- Factual claims are a distinct text class (writer-owned liability; must survive cutting without drift) — the tool must distinguish checkable assertion from opinion.
- CUTTING is a first-class phase (30-50% against a hard cap; the question is "what can die without the argument collapsing").
- Claim-strength calibration is a legitimate diagnostic axis (argument-specific).
- The tool's arc ends before the piece does: model "out of my hands / returned with foreign edits" explicitly.
- Doneness is deadline-relative; MORE REVISION ≠ BETTER (Krugman flatness) — anti-tinker grounding.

# Dossier C — Cognitive science of writing phases (skeptic's audit)

[Verbatim-abridged from the cognition agent, 2026-07-03. External-validity caveat: nearly all evidence = undergrads on 20-60min lab essays; multi-week fiction projects essentially unstudied.]

## Findings (with evidential weight)
1. Writing is recursive orchestration, not a pipeline (Flower & Hayes 1981) — STRONG as description of minute-scale interleaving; protocol analysis, not hypothesis test.
2. Hayes 2012 demoted planning/revision to activities: revision = writing applied to own text, guided by a TASK SCHEMA. MODERATE.
3. Knowledge-telling → knowledge-transforming (Bereiter & Scardamalia 1987, STRONG) → knowledge-crafting (Kellogg 2008, holds READER's representation; MODERATE, argued by analogy). Kellogg 1996: all subprocesses compete for one WM pool.
4. Incubation real but SMALL: Sio & Ormerod 2009 meta, d≈0.29; divergent tasks benefit most; UNDEMANDING filler beats rest; lab timescale = MINUTES. Licenses "take a walk", NOT "drawer the manuscript".
5. Sleep-and-insight mixed (Wagner 2004 vs Schönauer 2018 non-replication). Weak-to-moderate, task-specific.
6. **COOLING-OFF: thin, indirect, partly contradictory.** Chanquoy 2001 (children!): postponed revision → more/deeper revisions (framed as load relief). Daneman & Stainton 1993: authors detect FEWER errors in own weeks-old text than strangers' — deficit SURVIVED two-week delay (undercuts strong cooling claim at typo level). Mechanism that holds: PREDICTABILITY suppresses error detection; the author is the maximally-predicting reader of their own text. Construal-level route contaminated (Förster retractions). VERDICT: craft consensus with plausible converging mechanisms, no direct adult demonstration.
7. Revision expertise = TASK DEFINITION, teachable fast: detect→diagnose→fix (Hayes et al. 1987); surface-vs-meaning taxonomy (Faigley & Witte 1981, N=18); students revise lexically, experts globally (Sommers 1980, N=40) — foundational but tiny-N. **Wallace & Hayes 1991: EIGHT MINUTES of "revise globally" reframing changed revision behavior** — task schema, not ability, is the bottleneck.
8. Read-aloud: evidence for SURFACE error detection (Cushing & Bodner 2022, production effect); rhythm/structure claim UNTESTED.
9. Mode separation weaker than the poster version: Elbow believing/doubting = philosophy, not data. Nemeth 2004: criticism can INCREASE idea counts. What holds: **premature SURFACE polish taxes content generation** (Glynn et al. 1982: forced polished-first-pass → fewer arguments; Kellogg WM competition). "Never evaluate while generating" NOT supported.
10. Flow's dark side unshown; attachment comes from effort-justification (IKEA effect, Norton/Mochon/Ariely 2012, replicated) — cite that, never flow.
11. Time pressure: Amabile diary studies — creative thinking 45% less likely on high-pressure days + "pressure hangover"; exception = protected meaningful "mission" pressure. MODERATE (knowledge workers, correlational, working paper).
12. Yerkes-Dodson = folklore (Corbett 2015; mice, N=2-4/condition).
13. **Zeigarnik-as-memory REFUTED** (2025 meta, Nature HSSC); **Ovsiankina resumption-tendency survives**; Masicampo & Baumeister 2011: specific plan ELIMINATES unfinished-goal intrusions.
14. Implementation intentions d=0.65 (Gollwitzer & Sheeran 2006) — strongest effect in register. Attention residue (Leroy 2009) moderate. Days-scale warm-up costs: NO literature. "Write every day": contested (Boice tiny coerced samples vs Sword's productive binge-writers).

## Phase-model verdict
Gated stage model INDEFENSIBLE (recursion-at-minutes is the field's best result). Defensible: **phases as attractor states** — statistical dominance of one activity with all others available (van den Bergh & Rijlaarsdam functional-dynamic analyses: activity probability shifts with time-in-task, and the SAME activity correlates differently with quality depending on WHEN it occurs — the strongest empirical warrant for soft phases). Days-scale lifecycle = honest extrapolation only: soft, writer-declared, always overridable, framed as scaffolding not science.

## Folklore register (never present as research-backed)
"Rest it N days and see it fresh" · "write drunk edit sober"/strict mode lockout · Hemingway stop-mid-sentence (consistent w/ Ovsiankina, untested for quality) · "write every day" · Yerkes-Dodson · Zeigarnik memory · "read aloud reveals structure" · believing/doubting game · flow-as-craft-goal.

## Strop implications
1. FORBIDDEN: hard mode gates. Phases bias defaults/surfacing/framing, never lock.
2. DEMANDED: task-schema framing over ability framing — the cheapest high-evidence intervention is changing what the writer THINKS revision is at re-entry (Wallace & Hayes).
3. DEMANDED: honest labels on cooling-off (craft consensus, not "research shows"). Predictability mechanism justifies MAKING OWN TEXT LESS PREDICTABLE (different presentation, altered pacing, read-aloud) as evidence-adjacent alternatives to pure delay.
4. DEMANDED: plan-before-parting (d=0.65 + plan-quiets-the-loop) — the shipped intent ritual is the best-anchored feature in the product.
5. SUPPORTED: deferring SURFACE-level diagnostics during heavy drafting; deferring ALL evaluation is stance, not science.
6. SUPPORTED: altitude-aware revision (global-first nudge), disclosing tiny-N 1980s base.
7. CAUTION: incubation claims must match timescale (d≈0.29, minutes).
8. Attachment via IKEA effect; time-pressure warnings via Amabile as suggestive.

# Dossier D — Deadline pacing, progress displays, ADHD self-regulation (evidence-tiered)

[Verbatim-abridged from the pacing agent, 2026-07-03. Tiers: A meta/replicated · B solid single · C suggestive · D gray/lore · F folklore.]

## Findings
- PLANNING FALLACY at writing scale: thesis writers predicted 33.9 days, took 55.5; deadlines predict behavior (r=.82), plans don't (r=.23) (Buehler/Griffin/Ross 1994). Tier A. Boundary: underestimation is specific to LONG multi-day work (Halkjelsvik & Jørgensen 2012) — i.e., exactly a two-week piece.
- Self-imposed interim deadlines: demand is real (people pay for them) = Tier A; the PERFORMANCE BENEFIT FAILED preregistered replication (Hyndman & Bisin 2024 vs Ariely & Wertenbroch 2002); interim targets sometimes made performance WORSE (Burger et al. 2011). Verdict: overturned.
- Parkinson's law: thin real lab support for PACE only (Bryan & Locke 1967; Brannon 1999) — Tier B-C; organizational version F.
- Deadlines manufacture finished work: NSF dropped deadlines → proposals fell 59% (Tier B); deadline clustering universal incl. professionals.
- Time pressure vs creative quality UNSETTLED: Amabile "Creativity Under the Gun" = never-peer-reviewed working paper; 2023 meta codes effects near zero (r=−.02/−.06); inverted-U rejected outright (Song et al. 2023, N=15,751). Defensible weak claim only: high pressure does not reliably help. [NB: downgrades dossier C's "moderate" — use this weighting.]
- Goal gradient REAL and field-verified (Kivetz 2006, ~20% acceleration near reward) Tier A; pulls only when CLOSE (charity: only at ~85%).
- Endowed progress: one study (Nunes & Drèze 2006), no replication. Tier C.
- Stuck middle: suggestive (Bonezzi 2011, Tier C); actionable part = SMALL-AREA FRAMING (Koo & Fishbach 2012, Tier B): "20% done" early, "20% left" late.
- Subgoals help EARLY (attainability), BACKFIRE LATE: markers hurt when distance-to-goal is certain (Amir & Ariely 2008); late focus should shift to the superordinate goal (Huang 2017). Tier B. No creative-project study — extension is inference.
- Fresh starts double-edged: real (Beshears RCT) but resets HARM after strong prior performance (Dai 2018) and anticipated landmarks license present slacking (Koo 2021).
- Progress principle: right idea, Tier B-C evidence (peer-reviewed backbone = Amabile 2005 ASQ, correlational); setback asymmetry (2-3× stronger than progress) is trade-book tier but direction rides Tier-A negativity bias (Baumeister 2001). Weick = theory essay, cite as framing.
- STREAKS: motivate while intact, leave you BELOW BASELINE when visibly broken (Silverman & Barasch, 7 experiments, Tier B); worst when self-attributed; REPAIRABILITY blunts it. Converging: Duolingo forgiveness A/Bs; lapse-HCI (Epstein 2016: deficit framing alienates; Agapie 2016: pre-authorized "cheat points" sustain; UbiFit: achievement-only display).
- Self-forgiveness beats shame for resumption: Wohl 2010 (Tier C, unreplicated); self-compassion ρ=−.31 w/ procrastination (Sirois 2014, Tier B); shame→withdrawal, guilt/behavior-framing→repair (Tangney, Tier B).
- ADHD: time perception/reproduction deficit g≈0.4-0.7 (≥3 metas, Metcalfe 2024 g≈0.69) TIER A; delay discounting d=0.43, near-doubled for real rewards (motivational, not incapacity) TIER A; procrastination r≈.43 inattention-driven (Tier B). Hyperfocus under-defined (C); "wall of awful" = coach metaphor (D) over real task-initiation deficit (B); body doubling emerging-qualitative (C) — Zajonc actually predicts IMPAIRMENT on complex novel tasks; ICNU unvalidated packaging (D); visual timers: child RCTs d≈0.3-0.4, NO adult trial.
- Perfectionistic CONCERNS drive non-finishing (+.23 procrastination, Tier A); even self-oriented perfectionism → FEWER publications (Sherry 2010, N=1258 professors, Tier B); maximizing misery = exhaustive search + rumination, not high standards (Belli 2022 meta, Tier A); creative-cliff illusion: people quit GENERATION too early (Lucas & Nordgren PNAS 2020, Tier B). **No empirical literature on revision-stopping rules — genuine gap; Valéry's abandonment line (genuine, NRF 1933) is the field's entire canon.**

## Progress-display verdicts
MUST: make daily progress on the piece visible, setbacks rendered gently (quiet day = neutral, never loss) · small-area framing that CHANGES BY PHASE (done-so-far early, remaining late) · lapses repairable/cheap/task-framed ("the draft slipped", never "you failed") — strongest cross-tier triangulation in the review · externalize time itself for the ADHD writer (deadline perceptually present, not remembered).
NEVER: streak counters that zero · progress markers once the end is certain and near (late confetti breeds coasting) · mid-project resets/fresh-start prompts for a writer on pace · trusting the writer's schedule estimate (anchor to deadline + observed behavior) · shame surfaces (no red days, no deficit counts, no "you're behind" as identity).

## The 2-week shape (moderate confidence)
2-3 phases, not 14 daily quotas; milestone salience front-loaded, endgame deadline-anchored; assume ~1.5-2× the writer's drafting estimate and STRUCTURALLY PROTECT THE REVISION WINDOW (planning fallacy = best-replicated finding in the review); "draft fast revise slow" is defensible via planning-fallacy + Kellogg WM, NOT via pacing outcome studies (none exist); daily-touch held loosely (Sword: only 12.8% of exemplary academic writers write daily) — steady-contact-by-default, zero penalty for gaps.

## Folklore register
"Don't break the chain" (Seinfeld disavowed) · "self-imposed deadlines improve output" (failed replication) · "moderate pressure boosts creativity" (meta-rejected) · "draft fast revise slow" (no outcome studies) · "write every day or perish" · wall-of-awful/ICNU/body-doubling as proven interventions · 1667/day NaNo math (unstudied) · Matthews "written goals +33%" (press release) · "professionals don't procrastinate".

## Strop implications
- The deadline is the engine; Strop's job = keep it PERCEPTUALLY REAL across two weeks (Tier-A time-horizon deficit), not add motivational machinery.
- The one display with creative-work backing: evidence of TODAY'S progress on the piece itself; quiet days neutral.
- Every lapse-adjacent surface presumes return (repairable, achievement-only, task-framed) — same temperament as "reward during the session or not at all".
- Progress framing must CHANGE PHASE (done-so-far young → distance-to-deadline once end certain).
- Structurally distrust the writer's schedule; quietly protect the back half for revision.
- "Done" needs an external anchor (the submission) — no scientific stopping rule exists to borrow.
- Anything shipped here is AHEAD of the literature — borrow convergent principles, treat magnitudes unknown, let Strop's rig be the experiment.

# Dossier E — CST theory + lifecycle-aware tool landscape

[Verbatim-abridged from the CST/landscape agent, 2026-07-03.]

## CST theory verdicts
- Shneiderman genex (collect-relate-create-donate): explicitly NON-LINEAR, a designer lens not a user pipeline.
- Resnick et al. 2005 consensus: "support many paths and many styles" — essentially anti-imposed-workflow.
- CSI (Cherry & Latulipe 2014) measures exploration/expressiveness/immersion/enjoyment — NOT process compliance; scaffolding is good only insofar as it raises those.
- Premature convergence is tool-inducible; structure that helps = deliberate DIVERGENCE support, not sequencing. Double diamond: divergence→convergence cycles repeat FRACTALLY (per scene, per paragraph) — a one-global-divergent-phase model has the wrong resolution. What matters: not mixing modes at the same INSTANT (session-scale concern, not lifecycle-scale).
- EXPERTISE REVERSAL: scaffolds that rescue novices are extraneous load for experts (ReviewFlow 2024; NarrativeLoom 2026: experts prefer streamlined, novices structured).
- Compton casual creators license heavy scaffolding ONLY for autotelic creation; deadline/streak mechanics do the REVERSE conversion (turn autotelic writing into task-completion → NaNoWriMo damage).
NET: research supports mode discipline + optional ignorable scaffolds; does NOT support encoding lifecycle as tool structure. Structure helps at SESSION granularity, writer-invoked.

## Landscape (abridged)
- Scrivener: full project model; binder praised for rearranging, convicted as procrastination surface + per-project relearning; targets "healthy pressure" for some.
- Ulysses/iA: no imposed process — praised precisely for that.

## Deadline autopsy verdict
Deficit-displaying targets (fixed line, visible gap) demoralize and degrade quality; recalculating-forward targets + forgiveness mechanics retain motivation, shed shame. The deadline feature that survives contact with writers is A PLAN THAT BENDS, not a judgment that accumulates.

## Steal / refuse
STEAL: editorial-letter shape (batch, writer-summoned) · session-scale opt-in phase locks · bend-don't-shame pacing math · stage labels as vocabulary not gates (checkpoint stamps, zero behavioral consequence) · the graveyard folk pattern (cut text at bottom, notes at top of the ONE file — dignify it).
REFUSE: imposed pipelines · structure surface area (procrastination surface) · global lifecycle stages for a solo writer · deficit-displaying progress graphs · continuous evaluation during generation.

## Strop implications
- Don't model the lifecycle explicitly; derive it from what the writer did.
- The phase question is real but at the wrong scale as posed: the load-bearing distinction is per-session generate/evaluate (fractal), which the door + reveal clock ALREADY implement. That IS Strop's phase model; no project-level twin needed.
- Strop's AI is the convergent instrument — lifecycle sensitivity legitimately lives in pass WEIGHT (light passes vs editorial-letter heaviness), writer-invoked intensities, not inferred stages.
- Stage markers if ever: labels on checkpoints, zero gates.
- Support the graveyard INSIDE the single document; checkpoints already provide snapshot-courage.
- Deadline features: if ever, recalc-forward, days-off aware, no deficit display, forgiveness built in.
- Phase-lock only as writer-chosen session ritual (Freewrite-grade precedent).
- Unbundled-chain pain is Strop's quiet advantage — keep single-document local-first; export must never become Scrivener-compile.
