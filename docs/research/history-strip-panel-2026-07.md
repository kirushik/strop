# The Strop history strip, by the Δλ method

## The method, faithfully reconstructed (before applying it)

The Data Laboratory algorithm — **Δλ (дельта-лямбда)** — is Tanya Misyutina's step-by-step recipe for visualization, formulated from lab projects (Moscow Marathon, temperature-records calendar, World of Tanks, ПДД-trainer errors) and taught at [bureau.ru](https://bureau.ru/soviet/20240515/) and [datalaboratory.ru/new-course](https://datalaboratory.ru/new-course/):

1. **Describe the data reality (реальность данных)** — the real-world processes that generate the data; it supplies natural metaphors and exposes blind spots ([Δλ overview](https://bureau.ru/soviet/20240515/)).
2. **Identify the data particle (частица данных)** — "the minimal indivisible element… the building brick from which the whole data reality is built"; the unit of meaning (immigrant, runner, expense, tank) ([course lecture](https://bureau.ru/courses/data/algorithm/), [Шаг 2](https://bureau.ru/soviet/20240724/)).
3. **Materialize the particle on screen as a visual atom (визуальный атом)** — a graphic primitive whose visual channels (color, size, shape, opacity) carry the particle's properties; encoding must be **наглядным, различимым и аддитивным** — intuitive, distinguishable, and *additive*: atoms may visually sum, but the sum must decompose back into atoms ([visual atoms résumé](https://bureau.ru/soviet/20250625/), [main idea](https://blog.infotanka.ru/all/datalab-algorithm-main-idea/)). For rectangles, area must have physical meaning ([atoms, part 2](https://blog.infotanka.ru/all/datalab-algorithm-visual-atoms-2/)).
4. **Order the particles with a frame (каркас)** — rigid axes/guides organizing the plastic "mass of data" (масса данных) that "drapes over any frame"; macro picture from micro particles, never the reverse ([main idea](https://blog.infotanka.ru/all/datalab-algorithm-main-idea/)).
5. **Combine views** — all charts "slepped from the same dough": same particles, re-laid on different frames, so linked interaction is natural ([atom examples](https://bureau.ru/soviet/20241211/)).

Governing principles: **информативность, многомерность, наглядность** (informativeness, multidimensionality, vividness); "проявить данные — главная задача" — the point is to *develop* the data like a photograph; show all of it; sketch non-standard formats freely — "в топку круговые диаграммы! Дорогу удаву, плитке, мозаике, слоёным графикам, капиллярам" ([Визуализационное кунг-фу, 404fest 2013](https://2013.404fest.ru/infotanka/)).

## 1. My principles for this problem

1. **Find the particle before drawing anything.** Every question about two weeks of writing must reduce to operations on a set of particles ([Шаг 2](https://bureau.ru/soviet/20240724/)).
2. **The big picture is assembled from atoms, not painted over them.** If your session bar can't be broken back into keystrokes, you drew a lie ([main idea](https://blog.infotanka.ru/all/datalab-algorithm-main-idea/)).
3. **Encoding: наглядно, различимо, аддитивно.** Every channel must have a one-line physical meaning; summed area must mean summed work ([atoms, part 2](https://blog.infotanka.ru/all/datalab-algorithm-visual-atoms-2/)).
4. **The frame is honest and rigid; the mass is plastic.** Axes are real dimensions of the data reality — time and position — not decorative arrangements.
5. **Richness = density of meaning, not decoration.** The marathon map is an explosion of color because every fleck is a runner. Your founder's "explosion of color-coded abstraction" is exactly my aesthetic — earned pixel by pixel.

## 2. The v2 takes, through this lens

**Take A** aggregated before encoding: the particle (the op) was destroyed and replaced by an invented one (the "burst") with mystery height and a single-kind color that averages away the interleaving of writing and cutting. Macro without micro — the cardinal Δλ sin. **Take B** was the closest to right: correct frame (x=time, y=position), correct instinct, but *no atom design* — equal-sized dots throw away length (area means nothing), and localizing a whole-document AI pass as a dot at one height is dishonest about the particle's reality. Half-baked, as the founder said; the algorithm finishes baking it. **Take C** invented a mass with no particles at all — bands that decompose into nothing. Unshippable by definition.

## 3. The concept: «Ткань» (the Fabric)

**Data reality.** A writer knits a document through time. Every moment of work touches a *place* in the text. The AI reads the whole cloth; cards pin to threads; checkpoints are stations; restores are new rows knitted from an old pattern. The strip is the fabric of the manuscript, seen edge-on through time.

**Particles and atoms (precise):**

- **Text-op** (the master particle: one coalesced keystroke run). Atom: a **1-px-wide vertical fleck**. Channels: **x** = time; **y** = character position at that moment (document start at top); **height** = run length in characters (so *area = characters touched* — physical meaning); **color** = warm amber #C8A951 for inserts, a burnt/ashen amber (~#8A6D35) for deletes — both warm, both human, distinguishable by value; **opacity** accumulates additively where flecks overlap, so dense work *glows*. All text mass is warm: the AI never writes, and the fabric's color proves it.
- **Document envelope**: the derived length-over-time curve, a thin warm-white line bounding the mass. Caption: "the white line is how long your draft was at that moment." Restores make it visibly jump.
- **Pass**: a **full-height translucent cool-blue veil** at its timestamp (selection-scoped passes veil only that y-span). Honest: the AI read *everything*, so the mark spans everything. Width = read duration.
- **Card**: a **thin cool-blue horizontal thread** from (t-raised, anchor-y) to (t-resolved/dismissed) — the atom is a segment, the particle "two connected events" ([atoms, part 2](https://blog.infotanka.ru/all/datalab-algorithm-visual-atoms-2/)). Altitude = three blue lightness steps (dark=developmental, mid=line, light=copy). Terminal: sage dot = resolved, grey dot = dismissed; open threads run to the right edge.
- **Checkpoint**: frame, not mass — a full-height neutral **station tick** with its name set at rest in a dedicated label lane atop the strip. Collision policy: priority ladder (writer-named > draft-seal > manual > before-restore > export > ctrl+s > idle-gap); two staggered label rows with leader lines; losers keep their tick and a 3-ch abbreviated stub, full name at next zoom or on hover (hover only *expands* the visible stub).
- **Restore**: a **sage full-height tick** at the restore moment plus a **sage arc** over the label lane from source station to that tick. Right of it, the fabric continues — the append is visible; nothing to the right ever disappears.

**Frame and zoom grammar.** x = time, piecewise-linear: within sessions linear; gaps >15 min compress into a narrow stitched **seam** labeled at rest ("2 d"), expandable to exact dates. Three zoom levels, one rule — *only the x-scale changes, atoms never change identity*: **Project** (all sessions between seams; flecks sum into glowing columns), **Session** (one session; interleaved amber/ash stripes visible — mixed sessions look striped, never averaged), **Burst** (seconds; individual runs resolvable, card threads readable).

**At rest** the strip is a glance-answer: a warm fabric growing left to right, glowing where you worked, ash where you cut, blue veils where the editor read, named stations, sage arcs where you returned. **Scrubbing:** a warm-white playhead slides continuously (snapping across seams); mass right of it dims to ~35%; the document above live-renders. Any frozen frame reads as "bright = had happened by this moment" — the screenshot test passes structurally. Keyboard: ←/→ by op, Shift by burst, Tab between stations; two playmark pins allow side-by-side comparison of two moments.

**At 3× density** nothing is redesigned: additive opacity is the degradation law. Flecks fuse into brighter columns; the picture becomes *more* saturated, not noisier; zoom decomposes it back to atoms. That is what "аддитивно" buys you.

## 4. Make it so

1. **Give the strip real height (≥120 px).** The y-axis is the document; starve it and you're back to Take A.
2. **Render deletes as first-class ash-amber mass, interleaved at pixel level.** Never one color per session.
3. **Ship the restore arc + envelope jump together.** The append-not-erase promise must be legible in one glance.

## 5. What I refuse to ship

Any pre-binned "burst bar" as the base mark; equal-size dots; passes drawn as points; hover-only station names; a y-axis that is not honestly character position; any encoding needing more than one caption line; and any density "fix" that averages writing and cutting into a single hue. If the data must be thinned to look calm, the design has failed — проявляйте данные, не прячьте их.

== THE STROP HISTORY STRIP: A GRAPHICAL SCHEDULE FOR A MANUSCRIPT ==

**by way of Edward Tufte**

---

### 1. Principles

**First: above all else, show the data.** The data-ink ratio is not an aesthetic preference; it is a moral position. Every pixel in this strip should be an op, an event, or a coordinate — "erase non-data-ink" and "erase redundant data-ink" (*The Visual Display of Quantitative Information*, ch. 4–5). Graphical excellence "gives to the viewer the greatest number of ideas in the shortest time with the least ink in the smallest space" (*VDQI*, p. 51). Your founder wants an explosion of color. Good — but an explosion of *evidence*, not of decoration.

**Second: the Marey lesson.** The graphical train schedule Marey reproduced in *La Méthode graphique* (Paris, 1885) — devised, it appears, by the engineer Ibry — which I put in *VDQI* (p. 31) and on its cover, is the exact ancestor of this problem. Time runs across; position runs down; a train is a *line*, and the line's slope is speed, its extent is span, its crossings are meetings. Nothing is a dot, because nothing in a railway's day is instantaneous. A manuscript's life is a railway whose stations move: the document grows and shrinks. The plane is time × character-position, and every event has honest extent in both.

**Third: micro/macro — to clarify, add detail.** "Simplicity of reading derives from the context of detailed and complex information, properly arranged" (*Envisioning Information*, ch. 2). High density is the *friend* of the novelist, not the enemy: at arm's length she sees the shape of two weeks' work; leaning in, she sees Tuesday's cut. The same image must reward the glance and the expedition — that is your constraint 8, and it is exactly the micro/macro reading.

**Fourth: layering and separation, smallest effective difference.** On a dark ground, "1 + 1 = 3 or more" (*Envisioning Information*, ch. 3, after Albers) — every unnecessary contrast breeds visual noise. "Make all visual distinctions as subtle as possible, but still clear and effective" (*Visual Explanations*, p. 73). Muted deep grounds carrying thin bright data lines are the grammar of night aeronautical charts (*Envisioning Information*, ch. 5); your machine-room dark is well chosen — now earn it.

**Fifth: integrate words, numbers, graphics.** "Evidence is evidence" — completely integrate words, numbers, images, diagrams (*Beautiful Evidence*, "The Fundamental Principles of Analytical Design"). Station names, timestamps, and word counts live *on* the graphic, in type, at rest. A hover balloon is a confession that the design failed.

### 2. The v2 takes, briefly

**Take A** commits the classic sin: a visual dimension (bar height) carrying no data. Non-quantitative height is chartjunk, full stop. Worse, it imposes discreteness on a continuous record — as if Ibry had drawn his trains as a row of departure chips. And a bar that averages a mixed session into one color is not a simplification; it is a small lie.

**Take B** found the right plane and then betrayed it with the wrong marks. A dot has no duration, no extent, no magnitude — three lies per mark. And placing a whole-document AI pass at *a* height is meaningless, as your founder correctly smelled. The diagnosis is not "abandon the time-position plot"; it is "fix the marks." Draftback is Marey with the trains erased.

**Take C** falsified its vertical axis. When position isn't position, you have stolen the reader's spatial reasoning and given back a texture. Weirdness was the honest part.

### 3. The concept: the Manuscript Schedule

One design, three zooms, no mode switches. X is writing-time; Y is character position, top = document start.

**The envelope.** A thin cream line traces document length over time — the manuscript's skyline. Everything hangs inside it. Growth, cuts, and restores are legible in the silhouette alone; this line, collapsed to word-size, is also the strip's closed state — a true sparkline in the status bar, "data-intense, design-simple, word-sized" (*Beautiful Evidence*, ch. 2), inflating to the full schedule on open.

**One mark = one coalesced run of text ops** (same kind, contiguous, gaps under ~2 s). It is a *stroke*, never a dot: x-extent = duration; y-extent = span of positions touched; at burst zoom it slants from (t₀,p₀) to (t₁,p₁), so typing speed is slope — Ibry's trains, exactly. Color: solid warm amber = insertion; the same amber, hollow = deletion. One hue, one author; fill state says grow or cut. Caption: *"Solid amber, words written; open amber, words cut."* Alpha is additive, so overworked passages simply darken — density becomes tone, honestly.

**An AI pass is a translucent cool-blue column**, full height of the envelope (or the selection's y-range, if scoped), width = reading duration, altitude named in small type at its head ("line read"). It reads everything, so it *spans* everything — this repairs Take B's category error. Caption: *"A blue column is the editor reading; width is how long, height is what."*

**A card is a lifeline**: a 1-px cool-blue hairline at its anchor's y, from raised-t to closed-t, ending in a sage dot (resolved) or neutral-grey dot (dismissed); open cards run to the playhead. How long a diagnosis stayed open becomes visible length — a small multiple of the writer's responsiveness, free of charge.

**Stations.** A dedicated 14-px label row sits above the field, so names never occlude data (layering by separation). Checkpoints are full-height muted rules; names set at rest in small caps, right-set with leader ticks. Collision policy, explicit: (1) two-tier stagger; (2) beyond that, priority — writer-named > draft-seal > manual save > automatic — losers keep their tick and an ordinal, names recovered by zoom. Hover only *expands*: "Draft done" → "Draft done — Sat 12 Jul, 23:41."

**A restore is loud and sage**: a sage vertical rule at t-restore; the envelope visibly *steps* to the restored length; a thin sage arc in the label row reaches back to the source station, arrowhead pointing forward. Everything beneath the arc stays inked. Caption: *"A green arc reaches back; nothing under it is erased."* This is constraint 7 answered graphically, not verbally.

**Idle gaps** (>15 min) fold into narrow hatched seams labeled in small numerals ("18h", "3d") — a documented fold in the axis, not a hidden one (documentation is a fundamental principle of analytical design).

**Scrubbing.** The field is static; only a hairline playhead moves, with an integrated numeric readout — timestamp, word count — set in the label row. Time right of the playhead drops one alpha step. Every frame is a truthful still; the screenshot test passes because nothing performs.

**Zoom grammar.** Project → session → burst is pure x-dilation anchored at the playhead; y-mapping, marks, colors, and captions never change. Small-multiples discipline: variation in *data*, never in design (*Envisioning Information*, ch. 4). At project scale strokes fuse into fingerprint tone; at session scale runs separate; at burst scale slopes speak.

**At 3× density:** tone deepens, lifelines mist, labels obey the priority policy — and nothing aggregates into bars, ever. Saturation is a signpost saying "zoom in," and the zoom is one gesture away.

### 4. Make it so

1. **Make the envelope the hero.** The length-curve is the strip's skyline and its closed-state sparkline; every other mark hangs inside it.
2. **One mark, one sentence.** Every mark class ships with its one-line caption in the corner of the strip, set in type, at rest. If the caption can't be written, the mark can't be shipped.
3. **Give every event its true extent.** Duration on x, span on y, for ops, passes, and card lifelines alike. No dots.

### 5. What I refuse to ship

Hover-only meaning. Bars that average a mixed session into one color — a bar is a coffin with the detail buried inside. Any height that measures nothing. Animated transitions whose intermediate frames depict states that never existed. Glows, gradients, and reflections in the machine-room dark — the dark earns its color from evidence. And any view that elides the deletions: a history that flatters is not a history.

# The Long Exposure
*A response to the Strop history-strip brief — Bret Victor*

---

## 1. Principles

**Creators need an immediate connection to what they create.** ("Inventing on Principle," CUSEC 2012.) When you change something, you must see the effect immediately — no compile step, no "apply," no gap between gesture and consequence. Here the gesture is dragging the playhead, and the consequence is the document itself. You already have microsecond-cheap state recompute. That means the document *is* the tooltip. Every other decision in this strip is downstream of protecting that loop.

**Abstract over time first.** In "Up and Down the Ladder of Abstraction" (2011), the first rung of the ladder is abstracting over time — turning the car's motion into a static *trail*, so the whole behavior becomes a single visible object you can point at. A manuscript's history is exactly this: the trail the pen left. The strip should be the trail, and zoom should be the ladder — the same object seen at different altitudes, never a different object at each altitude.

**Information software is for answering questions, not for being interrogated.** ("Magic Ink," 2006.) The writer arrives with questions — *when did I gut chapter two? what did the editor say before I cut it? was Thursday productive?* — and the answers should be sitting there on the surface, readable like a printed graphic. Hover-to-discover is the interface confessing it hid the answer. Tufte is right about data-ink, and Magic Ink is his argument extended to dynamic media: density is a virtue when every drop of ink is a true sentence.

**Show the data *and* the behavior.** ("Stop Drawing Dead Fish," 2012; "Media for Thinking the Unthinkable," 2013.) A dot that says "a card was raised" is data. A thread that shows the card *living* — born, drifting as text moves around it, dying in sage when resolved — is behavior. Writers don't need a log; they need to see their own process as a thing with shape, so they can think about it. That is what makes this a medium and not a chart.

**Make time visible and tangible, and make comparisons possible.** ("Learnable Programming," 2012.) Scrubbing must be continuous, fine-grained, and reversible — and one state alone teaches little. The writer must be able to hold two moments side by side and see what changed between them.

## 2. The v2 takes, briefly

**Station Rail** commits the cardinal sin twice: a visual channel (height) with no meaning, and discreteness imposed on a continuous medium. If the mechanics let me stop *anywhere*, the picture must not say "you may only stand here." **Fingerprint Map** is the right species released as a fetus — the founder's instinct is correct. Its failures are all honesty failures: a whole-document read drawn as a point is a false statement; identical dot sizes erase the difference between a comma and a chapter. **Strata** decorates. Bands that aren't honestly position are drawings *about* the history, not the history.

## 3. The concept: a long-exposure photograph of the manuscript being written

The strip is one continuous picture — a long exposure. **X is time. Y is position in the document as it existed at that moment** (top of strip = first word, bottom = last). The document's changing length draws a cream contour — **the coastline** — rising through the dark #26251F as the draft grows, cliffing where you cut, and stepping back up in sage where you restored. The glance-story is free: growth, plateau, massacre, recovery.

**One mark = one coalesced op run** (a few seconds of continuous typing, or one deletion gesture).

- **x** — moment of the run
- **y + vertical extent** — the exact span of text touched; a pasted paragraph is a long stroke, a comma fix a fleck
- **color** — warm amber = words added; ash (drained grey) = words cut. One caption: *"amber is ink laid down, ash is ink taken away."*
- **shape** — inserts are solid strokes; deletes are hatched, reading as strike-through
- **opacity** — low, and marks *compose*: reworked passages glow brighter. Density becomes heat with no extra encoding.

Sessions therefore stay honestly composite — an evening of writing-while-cutting is visibly amber braided with ash, never averaged into one lying bar.

**AI passes are full-height translucent cool-blue washes** — the machine read *everything*, so the mark spans everything; a selection-scoped pass spans only its selection. Width = duration; a small glyph at the wash's top names the kind (believing / developmental / line / copy). This repairs Fingerprint Map's worst dishonesty.

**Cards are threads.** A raise is an open blue circle at (time, anchor); from it a one-pixel blue thread follows the anchor forward — *meandering as text above it is added and cut, which is true and quietly beautiful* — until it ends in a sage dot (resolved) or a grey tick (dismissed). Altitude = thread weight. Caption: *"blue threads are the editor's open questions; they end in sage when you answer them."*

**Stations.** Checkpoints are thin cream verticals with names set at rest in a single label lane along the strip's top, small caps. Collision policy, explicit: priority is writer-named > draft-seal > before-restore > manual > export > session-start > ctrl+s > idle-gap; when labels would collide at the current zoom, the loser demotes to a tick glyph — never overlapping, never deleted — and zooming in re-promotes it. Hover only *expands* ("Sealed" → "Sealed — Sat 12 Jul, 21:14, 9,802 words").

**Restore** is a sage seam — a full-height sage stroke at the restore moment, the coastline stepping to the checkpoint's length, and a thin sage arc drawn over the top of the strip from the source station to the seam. Everything left of the seam remains fully inked. Caption: *"you carried a checkpoint forward; nothing behind it was erased."* The picture itself repudiates the fear.

**Time folds, honestly.** Within a session, x is linear. Between sessions, the axis folds into a narrow dark gutter printed with its duration ("2 days"). The fold is visible at rest — compression declared, never smuggled.

**Scrubbing.** The playhead is a cream hairline carrying a chip — timestamp, word count. Dragging re-renders the document above at 60fps, viewport locked to the edit locus; everything right of the hairline drops to 35% opacity. Any paused frame reads as a complete statement: *this document, as of Saturday 14:02, 8,214 words.* Arrow keys step op-by-op at burst zoom, event-by-event with modifiers; brackets jump stations. **Pin a ghost playhead** to fix a second hairline: a delta chip appears (*"+1,204 words, 2d 3h"*) and the document view can show the interval's diff, amber and struck-ash inline. Two moments, side by side.

**Zoom grammar.** Continuous wheel zoom anchored at the playhead, with three landmark rungs — Project (weeks; strokes merge into weather, coastline dominates), Session (one sitting; bursts resolve into clusters, wash glyphs and demoted labels return), Burst (minutes; individual runs, scrub granularity = one op). The marks never change meaning across rungs — they only resolve. That is the entire grammar, and it is why trust survives the climb.

**At 3x density** nothing new is needed: opacity composition turns crowding into heat, the label lane sheds to ticks by the stated priority, threads bundle into a braid with a count chip ("14 open"). The coastline and the blue washes remain legible at any density because they are structural, not pointillist.

## 4. Make it so

1. **Scrub at frame rate or don't ship the strip.** The live document is the product; if the hairline ever stutters or snaps, the immediate connection is severed and the rest is decoration.
2. **One geometry, every rung.** Never re-encode across zoom levels. Zoom resolves; it never translates.
3. **Every channel earns one printed sentence.** Set the caption legend in the strip itself, tiny, visible at rest. A channel that can't earn its sentence gets deleted, not documented.

## 5. What I refuse to ship

Hover-only meaning — an interface that must be interrogated. Any mark with an unexplained channel ("mystery heights"). AI passes drawn as points — a whole-document read localized to a dot is a lie. Snap-to-chunk scrubbing — discreteness imposed on continuous time. A "play" button as the primary interface — spectating history instead of holding it in your hand. And any animation, however handsome, that fails the screenshot test — a frame that means nothing is a frame that lies.

# The record of the work: a note on the Strop history strip

*Jef Raskin*

## 1. Principles

**First, the Law.** "A computer shall not harm your work or, through inaction, allow your work to come to harm" — and the second: it shall not waste your time (*The Humane Interface*, 2000; see [the discussion of the laws](http://justaddwater.dk/2009/03/19/jef-raskins-first-law-of-interface-design-explained/)). Your append-only keystroke log is the first storage model in years I would bless without reservation. But the Law binds the *interface*, not merely the database: no gesture available anywhere near this strip may be capable of losing work, and none may need a warning dialog to be safe. Warnings are confessions.

**Second, modes and the locus of attention.** An interface "is modal with respect to a given gesture when (1) the current state of the interface is not the user's locus of attention and (2) the interface will execute one among several different possible responses to the gesture, depending on the system's current state" (*THI*, ch. 3; [definition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mode_(computer_interface))). So: *is* a bottom history strip a mode? It is a mode exactly to the degree that the system's "when" is not where the writer is looking. Do not fix this with a banner or a glowing border — peripheral state indicators fail under habituation; nobody sees the Caps Lock light. Fix it structurally: the document itself, the locus, must *be* the state (you already live-render it — good), and every gesture must mean the same thing at every moment in time.

**Third, quasimodes and habituation.** A mode maintained by a physically held key is not a mode in the harmful sense; muscle keeps what memory drops (*THI*, ch. 3 — the Canon Cat's LEAP keys, 1987). Habituated gestures will eventually be fired blindly; design so blind firing is always safe. And be monotonous: one grammar, identical at every zoom level. "Intuitive" merely means "familiar" (Raskin, *Intuitive Equals Familiar*, CACM 1994) — you earn familiarity by never varying.

**Fourth, quantify.** GOMS the core errand: "show me this paragraph as it stood yesterday morning." Count keystrokes and mental operators against the folk alternative (Save-As archaeology). If your strip loses, redesign it (*THI*, ch. 4).

**Fifth, zoom; never page.** Structure should be *derived*, never imposed — the Cat had one long text, not files; Strop has one long history, not versions. Whole-project to burst must be one continuous zoom about the point of interest, as in ZoomWorld (*THI*, ch. 6), with the encodings re-aggregating honestly, never swapping to a different picture.

## 2. The v2 takes

**Station Rail** discretizes a continuum — imposed chunks are little modes of time, and hover-only labels are invisible semantics; both violations. Single-kind bars *average away the truth*, and averaging is information destruction. **Fingerprint Map** commits the graver sin: a channel that means something only sometimes. If y is position-in-document, then a whole-document pass has no y; drawing it as a dot at a height teaches the writer to distrust the axis everywhere. **Strata** is decoration. If you cannot caption a mark in one line, you have not designed it; you have painted it.

## 3. The concept: one ribbon, five honest channels

At rest the strip is a single dark ribbon. On it:

- **The ink line.** A thin warm-white line whose height is document length over time. Caption: *"The line is how long your story was at each moment."* Its slope tells the growth story at a glance; a novelist reads it like a pulse.
- **One mark = one coalesced run of ops.** x = when; width = duration; side = above the ink line for insertion, below for deletion; height = characters per second; color = amber, both sides (cutting is the writer too — position disambiguates, so the colorblind lose nothing). Caption: *"Above the line you were writing; below, cutting; taller is faster."* A session that interleaves both shows both — nothing averaged.
- **AI pass = full-height translucent cool-blue column** spanning the pass duration. A pass reads everything, so its mark touches everything; no dishonest dot at a height. Caption: *"A blue column: the editor read the whole draft."*
- **Card = a blue thread** on one of three thin top sub-rails (developmental highest), running from raised to resolved; end cap sage if resolved, grey if dismissed; an open thread runs to the right edge. Caption: *"Each thread is one question, from asked to answered."*
- **Checkpoint = full-height hairline + its name set in small type at rest** on a label rail beneath. Collision policy, deterministic: writer-named > draft-seal > before-restore > manual > export > session-start > idle > ctrl+s; two staggered label rows; losers keep their tick plus a one-letter stub; zoom restores the rest. Tab steps station to station and speaks each name at the playhead — labels are never hover-gated; hover may only expand ("day 12" → "Sat 12 Jul").
- **Restore = sage, and visibly an append.** At the restore moment the ink line steps to the restored length, and a sage bridge arcs back to the source checkpoint. Everything left of it remains. Caption: *"A sage bridge: you brought an old version forward; nothing was thrown away."* This picture *is* the repudiation of destructive-restore fear.
- **Gaps >15 min collapse to a fixed perforated grey seam.** Dead air carries no information, but never splice silently — show the seam.

**While scrubbing**, the locus of attention is the *document* — the strip is read peripherally, by position and color patch only. Everything precise rides the playhead as one caption block: *"Sat 12 Jul 09:41 · Session 7 · 8,412 words · after 'Draft complete.'"* Every mid-drag frame is just ribbon + playhead: it passes the screenshot test because nothing is transient.

**The grammar (quasimodal, monotonous).** Hold the History key: the strip rises. While held — drag or Left/Right scrubs continuously; Shift+arrows jump station to station; Up/Down zooms about the playhead; and **typing LEAPs through time**: type a phrase and jump to the moment it was born; again, to its next change or its death. Search history by content, not date arithmetic — LEAP on the time axis. *Release the key and you are back at now, nothing changed.* Enter while held parks you in the past to read at leisure. Parking is not a hidden mode, because typing means one thing at every moment in this product: *your keystroke appends to the document as shown.* Typing while parked therefore first appends the restore (the sage bridge draws itself) and then your insertion. No dialog. It cannot destroy — nothing is truncated; it cannot surprise — what you see is exactly what you are committing to, and the pre-restore state is itself one leap away.

**Zoom registers** (continuous, always about the playhead, same encodings re-binned): *Project* — seams collapsed, per-pixel columns aggregate runs into amber-above/amber-below rate areas, only writer-named and seal stations labeled, card threads merge into a counted blue band. *Session* — one session fills the strip, all stations labeled, threads individual. *Burst* — seconds; the playhead caption grows a one-line diff (*+ "the harbor was empty"*). At 3× density nothing overplots, because aggregation *is* the encoding — areas, never dot clouds.

## 4. Make it so

1. **Make typing mean one thing everywhere.** Restore-by-typing, sage bridge, and not one confirmation dialog in the entire strip.
2. **Make History a held quasimode.** Release always returns to now with nothing changed; blind firing is always safe.
3. **Put LEAP on the time axis.** Writers remember sentences, not timestamps.

## 5. What I refuse to ship

Hover-only meaning of any kind. A confirmation dialog on restore. A read-only "viewing mode" with a locked keyboard — a dead keyboard is an error state wearing a costume. Snap-to-chunk scrubbing. Any height without a one-line caption. A second way to restore (menu *and* strip *and* dialog — monotony forbids it). And a modal "history view" that replaces the document: the writer's work stays on screen, sovereign, with the machine room humming honestly below it.

# The History Strip as a Viewspec — a response

*Douglas C. Engelbart*

## 1. Principles

**First: there is one record, and many portrayals of it.** In NLS we learned early that the file and the view of the file are different things, and that the leverage lives in the difference. A viewspec was not a convenience; it was a first-class part of the command grammar — single letters that clipped levels, truncated lines, filtered content, so that `x` showed you the bare top-level skeleton, one line per statement, and `w` gave you everything ([Engelbart & English, "A Research Center for Augmenting Human Intellect," AFIPS FJCC, 1968](https://www.dougengelbart.org/content/view/140/); see also the [keyset-era account of viewspecs](https://www.software-artist.com/keyset/)). Your oplog is the record. The strip is a viewspec over it. Every design question you have asked — what is a mark, what does zoom mean, what shows at rest — is a viewspec question, and should be answered in grammar, not in pictures.

**Second: the record is sacred and append-only.** Our Journal, running from 1970, made every submitted document a permanent, read-only entry — you could never delete, only add ([Engelbart, Watson & Norton, "The Augmented Knowledge Workshop," AFIPS NCC, 1973](https://dougengelbart.org/content/view/133/); [CHM on the ARC Journal](https://computerhistory.org/blog/sri-arc-journal-a-record-of-engelbart-and-his-team/)). Your restore-as-append is the Journal's ethic exactly. Honor it visibly.

**Third: the view changes the thinker.** In the 1962 framework I argued, extending Whorf, that the structure of our symbol-manipulating means shapes what we can think ("Augmenting Human Intellect: A Conceptual Framework," SRI Summary Report AFOSR-3223, 1962). A writer who can *see* her own process acquires a new literacy: she becomes capable of improving not just the draft (A-work) but her method of drafting (B-work), and eventually her method of improving (C-work) — the improvement hierarchy from my 1992 "Toward High-Performance Organizations." The strip is not nostalgia apparatus. It is bootstrapping apparatus.

**Fourth: capability over ease.** If ease of use were the supreme criterion, we would all still ride tricycles. A learnable grammar that repays study for years beats an unlearnable simplicity. Dense, keyboard-driven, explicable — your founder's "explosion of color-coded abstraction, still navigable" is precisely what a trained user of a high-performance workshop should see.

## 2. The v2 takes, through this lens

**Take A** confuses the portrayal with the record. It hard-codes one level of abstraction (the burst) as *the* view, with no level control — a viewspec system with one viewspec. Its single-kind bars average a composite session into a lie; a view that cannot show interleaving is a false record. Hover-only labels put the meaning outside the still.

**Take B** has the honest instinct — time × position is a truthful portrayal of the text-op stream — but it commits a category error: an AI pass is a statement about the *whole record*, and drawing it as a dot at one height asserts a locality that does not exist. And it ships one mark for every atom kind, with no filtering, no level clipping, no jumps. It is a view without viewspecs: half a system.

**Take C** fails the first test of any portrayal: it cannot express part of the record (restores). Discard.

## 3. The concept: the strip as the default portrayal of the writer's Journal

**The base fabric.** X is time, with idle gaps >15 min compressed but *marked* — an explicit gap glyph, "⋯ 2 d", labeled at rest; honest nonlinearity, never silent. Y, inside a single band, is position-in-document, top of band = top of document; the band's outer boundary is the derived document-length envelope in muted grey — the growth curve, the record's own first line.

**One mark = one coalesced op-run.** X-extent: its time interval. Y-extent: its character span, scaled to document length at that moment. Color: warm amber fill for insertion, ink-dark stroke for deletion. Caption: *"Amber is words arriving; ink is words leaving; the grey envelope is the document's length."* Because runs are drawn individually, a composite session shows as interleaved amber and ink — constraint 4 satisfied structurally, not cosmetically.

**A pass is a wash, not a dot.** Each AI pass renders as a translucent cool-blue wash spanning its full time interval and the *full band height* (a selection-scoped pass washes only its y-span). The whole-document read looks like what it is: the machine's gaze over everything. Pass kind (believing/developmental/line/copy) is a one-word label set inside the wash at rest, eliding to an initial at density.

**A card is a diamond** at (t, anchor-y), cool blue; size encodes altitude (developmental largest); open outline when raised, sage-filled when resolved, grey when dismissed.

**Stations.** Checkpoints are full-height hairline ticks rising into a dedicated label register above the band, names set in small type *at rest*. Collision policy, explicit: two staggered label rows with leader lines; beyond that, elide by priority class (writer-named > draft-seal > before-restore > manual > export > session-start) into a "+3" cluster glyph that expands on *zoom*, never hover. Hover may only unabbreviate ("Draft ✓" → "Draft complete · Sat 12 Jul").

**A restore is an arrival, not an erasure.** Sage tick at the restore moment; a thin sage arc riding over the label register, pointing *back* to the source station; the length envelope visibly steps to the restored size — and the record continues rightward, untruncated. One caption: *"Sage means something came back; nothing was destroyed."*

**Scrubbing.** The playhead is a cream hairline; everything to its right dims to a "not yet" opacity. This is a static encoding of the playhead's position, so any frame is a legal still (constraint 6). The readout above it: "Tue 14 · 3,412 words · after *Draft complete*." Press `,` to pin a second playhead; the readout becomes a delta ledger (+612 words, 2 cards resolved) — two moments comparable side by side (constraint 2). The playhead stops anywhere; zoom changes the portrayal's grain, never the scrub's.

**Zoom is level-clipping, not magnification.** Three derived levels — Project (sessions as statements), Session (bursts), Burst (individual runs) — navigated by viewspec, echoing NLS:

- `a` / `b` — clip one level deeper / shallower
- `w` — everything on; `x` — barest view (envelope + stations + restores)
- `t` `p` `c` `k` `r` — toggle text-ops, passes, cards, checkpoints, restores; uppercase solos a kind (the content-analyzer instinct, one letter each)
- `j s`, `j r`, `j p`, `j g`, `j c` — Jump to next station, restore, pass, gap, unresolved card (shift reverses)

The active viewspec letters are echoed in the strip's corner ("w·session") — state visible in every still — and the whole view serializes as (viewspec string, zoom anchor, playhead time): shareable, bookmarkable, reproducible. **At Project level, every session gets a first line**, a derived summary statement: "Tue 14 · 47 min · +1,204 −310 · 1 pass · 3 resolved." The project reads as an outline of the campaign. This is where co-evolution happens: the writer discovers she cuts best in the morning, that her restores follow line-editing binges, that resolved cards cluster after believing passes — and she changes her method. The tool improves the writer who improves the tool-use.

**At 3× density, one merge rule, stated once:** when two marks would overlap, they merge and their count survives. Runs fuse into amber/ink intensity (opacity ∝ ops per pixel-column — ink proportional to data, which is Tufte satisfied without poverty); diamonds stack into "◆5"; labels elide by the priority policy. Nothing silently vanishes.

## 4. Make it so

1. **Make the strip a pure function of (record, viewspec, playhead).** No hidden state, no unserializable view. If a still cannot be reconstructed from those three, the design has leaked.
2. **Give every session a first line.** Derived summary statements at Project level, so a glance reads the campaign the way `x` read a file.
3. **Put the grammar in the writer's hands and echo it.** Single-letter viewspecs, Jump commands, visible letter-echo. Ten minutes to learn; years of repayment.

## 5. What I refuse to ship

Hover-borne meaning. Single-kind session bars. Zoom that is magnification without level structure. Any portrayal that cannot express every atom kind in the record. And above all: a "simple mode" that amputates the grammar to flatten the learning curve. We do not shrink the workshop to fit the apprentice; we train the apprentice to inhabit the workshop. That is what augmentation has always meant.

# The history strip — notes from Ilya Birman

## 1. Principles

**The strip is a map, and a map has one bold line.** When Pasha Omelekhin and I drew the [Ekaterinburg metro map](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2016/06/map-design-redesigning-ekaterinburgs-metro-map/), the entire composition was one decision: a bold black route over quiet geography — streets, the Iset, the parks all present, all subordinate. Density was never the enemy; *undifferentiated* density is. You may show everything if the layers are ranked by contrast ([more on transport maps](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2017/07/transportation-map-design/)).

**Anchor objects.** A reader scans a surface by its anchors — the most noticeable objects form the entry points and the hierarchy ([«Якорные объекты», bureau.ru advice](https://bureau.ru/soviet/20140324/); the same rule I teach in the bureau's layout course). A panel with no anchors is texture; a panel that is all anchors is noise. The strip needs exactly one class of anchor: the stations.

**Interface is language; the control is its own indicator.** In [«Пользовательский интерфейс»](https://bureau.rocks/books/ui-en/) I insist that the words are the interface, and that state must be readable off the very thing you manipulate — not off a tooltip that appears when you breathe on it. A playhead that shows nothing until hovered is a broken instrument.

**Honest channels.** If a mark has a height, the height means one thing, always, and I can say it in one sentence. A dot placed "at a height" for compositional reasons is a small lie, and small lies compound into a diagram nobody trusts.

## 2. The v2 takes

**Take A** is a metro map where station names appear only when you touch the glass. Absurd stated that way, and it is that way. The bars also commit the worse sin: they impose discreteness on a continuous mechanism. If I can stop at any keystroke, do not show me lozenges.

**Take B** had the only honest idea of the three — y as position in the text — and then betrayed it by placing non-positional events (a pass reads *everything*) at a position. The founder is right that it's half-baked; the axis is worth keeping, the dots are not.

**Take C**: bands whose vertical extent means nothing are decoration. Неаккуратно — and inaccuracy of meaning is worse than inaccuracy of pixels.

## 3. The concept: the Envelope Line

One coordinate system, stated in one caption: **"Across — your working time. Up — the length of the story; anything drawn at a height sits at that place in the text."**

**The anchor: the envelope.** A single bold cream line (#FBFAF8 — literally the color of the page above) tracing document length over time. On the dark #26251F panel it is the metro route: the story itself, growing, plateauing, being cut. It is drawn as it is — stepwise at high zoom, no smoothing. This line is the strip's only bold object.

**The geography: edit texture under the envelope.** The space under the line *is* the document — length in characters. Fill it with a low-contrast density field of ops: amber (#C8A951) where the writer inserted, muted grey (#8A8678) where she deleted, at the true (time, position) of each coalesced run. A session that interleaves writing and cutting shows amber and grey braided — nothing averaged away (constraint 4). At 3× density the field simply saturates darker; a density field is built for summation and cannot overflow.

**Passes: columns, not dots.** An AI pass reads the whole document, so it occupies the whole height: a translucent cool-blue (#3D5C8C) column, width = the pass's real duration, capped floor-to-envelope. "The editor read all of this, during this time" — the encoding says exactly what happened. Pass kind labels appear from session zoom.

**Cards: dots at their true place.** Raised = small blue dot at (time, anchor position) — under the envelope, where they belong, usually clustered inside or after a pass column. Resolved = the dot's later sage (#7D8C66) twin; dismissed = the dot hollowed. At project zoom, nearby card dots merge into short vertical strokes; they separate again as you zoom. No count badges.

**Stations.** Checkpoints sit *on* the envelope line as cream circles — stations on the route, exactly as on a transit map. Named ones (writer-given, draft-seal, export) carry their names at rest in a label lane above the strip, set in the writer's own words: "Draft complete", never "checkpoint-7". Automatic ones speak human: "Started", "Break", "Saved" — no "idle-gap", no "ctrl+s". **Collision policy: ranked omission, never truncation.** Rank: writer-named > seal > export > session-start > save. A label that doesn't fit at this zoom is omitted whole (its station stays as a tick); zooming in readmits labels by rank — the same rule by which a city map reveals street names.

**Restores.** At the restore moment the envelope line steps vertically to the old length — a visible cliff — marked with a sage station and a thin dashed sage arc reaching back to the source checkpoint. Everything before the cliff remains drawn. The picture itself says: nothing was destroyed, a new edit was appended. That is the whole anti-fear argument, made without a single word.

**Time and its wording.** Two weeks of clock is mostly emptiness; collapse gaps >15 min into slim seams, so x is *working* time. Beneath the strip, day labels: "Today", "Yesterday", then "Tue 12 Jul". Not "day 12" — the writer does not number her days; the tool should not number them for her. Hover may expand "Tue 12 Jul" to "Tue 12 Jul, 19:02–23:14" — expansion of the visible, never birth of the invisible.

**Scrubbing.** The playhead is a full-height hairline carrying a permanent readout: "Tue 12 Jul, 21:40 · 8,214 words." The control is the indicator. It stops anywhere — the geography under it is continuous, so no snapping exists to fight. Any frozen frame is a map with a "you are here" line: the screenshot test passes by construction.

**Zoom grammar, one sentence:** zoom separates marks and readmits labels by rank; it never changes what any mark means. Three named levels — Project (stations + envelope + pass columns), Session (bursts as texture clumps, individual cards, minor ticks), Burst (stepwise envelope, individual runs) — same encodings at all three.

## 4. Make it so

1. **The envelope line is the only bold object on the strip.** Anything competing with it gets dimmer until it stops competing.
2. **Every mark's height is characters or it has no height.** One caption line for the whole strip; if a new feature can't live inside that caption, it doesn't go on the strip.
3. **Labels at rest, collisions by ranked omission, dead time collapsed to seams.** Hover only elaborates.

## 5. What I refuse to ship

Hover-born station names. Bars, chips, lozenges — any imposed discreteness over a continuous oplog. Dots for whole-document events. "Day 12". A smoothed envelope (the steps are the truth). Cluster badges, glows, and any animation whose intermediate frame needs an apology. The founder wants an explosion of color-coded abstraction — good; an explosion is interesting only when every fragment can testify to what it is.
