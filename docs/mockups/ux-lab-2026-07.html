The customs officer had a theory about the fog, and he told it to anyone who would listen: that it wasn’t weather at all, but the river forgetting its banks, one memory at a time. He had charts. He had dates. Nobody would listen.

“You’re not on the manifest,” Mara said. The stowaway looked down at his two shadows as if counting them. “Neither is the river,” he said.

The fog came up the channel at the hour the schedule board called nothing at all, and the running light made a room of it, small and white, moving with the boat.

On the fourth night she brought him coffee, because the body decides these things before the argument is over. He held the cup in both hands as if warming someone else’s fingers.
