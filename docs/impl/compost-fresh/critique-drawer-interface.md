# Critique — "Scraps: the drawer at the writer's left hand"

Panel verdict: **needs-surgery**. Baseline: **beats** — F1, F2, F5 are
structural wins no divider convention can match, and the third-region
bet independently re-derives the house's own unpublished conclusions.
What must die is the drawer's interior grammar, not its existence.

## Ilya Birman — interface as grammar

The structural core is right, and the blind designer re-derived several
standing decisions: a third top-level region versioned per checkpoint
state (07-compost.md §3.B, N3), "Set aside" as the only printed form of
the verb (ux-glossary.md), and separate verbs for to-caret vs to-origin
retrieval — Take back / Put back is lawful under P8 for exactly the
reason 07-compost.md R4 recorded: one word for two destinations breaks
the grammar. Convergence under blindness is evidence for the shape.

Then the interior contradicts the constitution's oldest wound. Entries
"under a small drained provenance row" carrying dates and a Put back
button are **per-item chrome at rest on the writer's editable text** —
the pattern asides.md §5 refuses outright, for the reason P3 records:
round 4 found "things on the left are not the cards; probably just
paragraphs." asides.md §1's entire data model — one continuous text,
blank-line separators — exists because it makes every structural
question answer itself: merge is backspace, split is Enter, reorder is
cut/paste. The drawer re-opens each. Test: select from mid-entry across
a provenance row into the next entry and cut. What carries the row?
What re-synthesizes it on paste? Either the chrome becomes text (the
blank-line model, drawn badly) or entries become objects (a widget
list; P3 dead).

Second sin: the record's anatomy worn by living text. The graveyard's
row-and-recede grammar is lawful there because the graveyard is a
record — machine's shape, read-only. Importing it wholesale asserts
parallel forms where meanings diverge — the exact "two card families"
confusion asides.md §0's table was written to dissolve. And the piles
run time in opposite directions: scraps newest-at-top, graveyard
appending at the tail. One afterlife where time flows up on the left
and down at the bottom fails P8 on its face. Prior art decided
append-only, arrival order, never a maintained sort (07-compost.md B4;
asides.md §1's tail anchor) — the reason being that text grows downward
and the *writer*, not the tool, curates order.

## Jef Raskin — modes and habituation

The designer names the disease in his own loss #5 — "the most modal
object in the product" — then ships it. Count the states remapping the
same gestures: capture line, drawer-focused, manuscript-focused. Esc
means commit-and-close, close-and-restore, or its three existing
meanings; ctrl-f means two different searches — all resolved by a focus
stack whose only indicator is a hollow caret. Habituation forms one
reflex per key; these reflexes will fire in the wrong state.

The sharpest mode error is the chord itself. Ctrl-shift-a means park,
jot, or take back depending on selection and focus. The killing case:
the writer intends a jot, but a forgotten selection sits in the prose —
the chord silently rips that passage out of the manuscript. The
compliance signal is a count ticking on a slim rotated sliver at the
extreme left edge; at 1600 pt that is sub-threshold peripheral vision.
F1's own requirement — "compliance is visible from where the writer
sits" — is the test, and the spine fails it at precisely the widths the
design calls its best case. (Prior art's answer: the near, pulsing
footer chip — the established file-without-travel idiom, 07-compost.md
N4.)

Also: the yielding law makes click-into-prose close the drawer at
800 pt and leave it open at 1600 pt — one gesture, two outcomes,
switched by window geometry the writer isn't watching. That is a mode.
Esc-commits-the-jot is house law (commit-not-abandon, R6), but its
inverse leaves by a different door: an accidental jot is undone by
opening the drawer and deleting there — P13 stretched thin.

## Don Norman — first contact, minute by minute

Minute one: no spine exists — correct, and convergent with asides.md §1
(an empty region has no referent; no false affordance). She writes.
Minute nine: she selects a limp paragraph to delete; the menu offers
Set aside beside the kill. The affordance rests exactly at the
hesitation — this is the design's best moment and a genuine F8
mechanism the tail-section prior art lacks.

Minute ten breaks it. She clicks Set aside. The paragraph ghosts out of
her prose — and the receipt is a sliver she has never seen, appearing
at the far edge, its label rotated 90°, while her eyes are on the wound
in her text. Nothing pulses near her gaze. First reading: *deleted*.
Does ctrl-z bring it back? The spec never says — park's undo story is
absent, and P13 demands she infer the inverse. She will not travel to a
spine she hasn't noticed to find Put back.

The jot: the capture line appears "at the caret's height" but slides
from the left edge — she must throw her eyes across the screen to watch
her own typing, mid-sentence. F2's near-zero-flow claim dies in that
eye travel. The field belongs at the caret; the motion toward the spine
belongs after commit. And the design concedes its invitation is one
thread wide: a backspace-deleter never meets the verb, and
hidden-until-first-use navigation is the pattern its own citation says
halves discovery.

## Panel — collisions, grafts, sentence

Re-invented failures: **(1)** two navigation idioms for the two
afterlives — spine-left vs chip-bottom is the same split the extraction
audit marked F6 ✗ ("different navigation idioms — panel vs footer bar,"
07-compost.md §2); the self-score "matches" is generous when the
baseline's pile sits physically above the graveyard in one scroll.
**(2)** Recede-with-age on the writer's *editable* pile is auto-tidying
— refused in asides.md §5 because the scrap box is hers and the tool
never wants (P2); the hunted months-old seed shown as a tool-collapsed
one-liner taxes F3. **(3)** The drawer is parked option C in a
slide-out costume: a second independent text flow (caret, focus, find,
Esc surgery ×2) fighting the ~35ch measure floor at 800 pt — the two
named reasons C was parked (07-compost.md §3.C), unanswered because
unseen.

Grafts, even if the drawer dies: the no-travel jot capture (relocated
to the caret) as the concrete "New scrap" mechanism; the name
**Scraps** — folk, warm, likelier than «компост» to survive the Russian
border the glossary worries over; the severity-gradient menu (annotate
/ set aside / exile) as the F8 invitation at the moment of hesitation;
focus-scoped find with the cross-scope tail row as data-plus-action;
the count control carrying its scope label the moment a second scope
exists (P12).

Against the null baseline: beats, honestly. Against the house's own
draft alternative: weakest where it is most confident. Surgery: keep
the verb, the invitation, the region model; replace the interior with
the blank-line text model; put the receipt near the gaze; give the two
afterlives one navigation idiom and one direction of time.
