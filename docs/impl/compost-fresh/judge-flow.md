# Judge — flow lens

Lens: the working writer's adoption and flow-cost. Which design is still in use in month three? Weighting by the evidence: park is the whole game (permission consumed at cut-time); jot cost mid-burst is near-sacred; the end-of-revision sweep is the one ritual that keeps a pile alive; retrieval is ~1–2% and phrase-grained. Designs judged as critiqued — unanswered kill-shots count.

## Ranking

**1 · tail.** The only design whose month-three case is structural, and its own practice critique says so: "it was in use before the feature shipped." It formalizes the incumbent practice instead of competing with it — the writer's pile, her ⌘F, her scroll-past sweep (geography restores the ritual for free: the graveyard sits below the scraps). Park is one chord, zero travel, invitation on the selection menu at the hesitation moment; the jot is org-capture-shaped; the empty state is pixel-identical to the baseline, so there is nothing to abandon. Its kill-shots are real but all are spec gaps with named fixes, several supplied by its own critiques: unspecified Ctrl-Z (add undo-first inverse), Esc teleporting a live caret out of editable scrap text (excursion latch — a solved house problem), the one-chip regression (descending chip), silent park tick (arrival feedback), provenance-chip colony (quiet provenance). Both panels' verdict — "surgery is subtraction and spec-completion, not redesign" — is what a flow winner under adversarial review looks like.

**2 · spartan.** Same geometry, same adoption inheritance, and the leanest resting footprint in the field — but two of its three flow-critical moments fail as specced. The jot transits the prose (type, select, chord: three gestures where the gold standard costs one, and a lull checkpoint can immortalize the grocery line as manuscript) — the worst F2 in the batch on the near-sacred flow. Park compliance is a decrement, the Spike's documented cause of death; the critique's corridor prediction (she scrolls down to verify) is a failed F1. And the magic string is a trust bomb: a legal line of dialogue silently re-scopes the manuscript; one deleted line silently dissolves the boundary. The grafts that repair it converge on… the tail design.

**3 · fold.** Owns the single best capture mechanism anywhere in the field — chord, type, Esc, caret restored to the letter, screen never moves — and zero resting chrome. But the parking tense, the whole game, fails at documented volume: at Pressfield ratio the draft striates with pleats, the only clean view is read-only, and the cure (curate folds to the graveyard) is exactly the maintenance the research says writers never perform. The Novelist's month-three line is disqualifying for the core flow: "I stop folding cuts around week five and go back to the bottom pile." The retype-gesture kill-shot (selection over a pleat silently routes living text to the dead record) attacks ordinary drafting. As critiqued, its aside half wins and its parking half loses.

**4 · drawer.** Park and the severity-gradient invitation are genuinely good, and parking keeps working in month three — permission is served even unvisited ("even if I never do"). But the body is the most modal object in the product: chord-only undiscoverable jot (Spike precedent), the stale-selection ambush (a jot silently parks a forgotten selection, then types into the manuscript mid-burst), Enter chopping thoughts, width-dependent click behaviour, focus-scoped find, and a far-edge receipt invisible at exactly its best widths. The removed scroll-past ritual is replaced with nothing; the critique's instrumentable prediction — park-rate up, open-rate collapsing by month three — plus its own rent audit (an in-flow tail region keeps every win but side-by-side) place it here.

**5 · margin.** Fails precisely the highest-value flow moments it was built for. The permission function inverts — the darling's opening phrase stays pinned beside the paragraph being rewritten without it, and the predicted regression is delete-instead-of-park, recreating the failure F1 exists to fix. The core "beside the seam it left" claim collapses under the load the feature invites (ten parks, one viewport, rationed lane). No gathered pile, so the sweep ritual is a full-document patrol while the dead record reads contiguously — perverse. Jot has no floor (bare-caret ⌃M isn't even lawful today). The boundary-reuse insight is the field's cheapest F5 and must survive as a principle; the body should not.

**6 · verso.** The only fatal interface verdict, and rightly under this lens: wrong-face drafting silently exiles manuscript prose from export, counts, AI and cold read — the most expensive flow failure in the field, discovered at deadline or never, guarded only by passive cues. Face-local find breaks the one documented retrieval mechanism ("never truly lost" reads as false exactly once). The sweep costs two flips per candidate with the gap held in memory. Excise the flip and the remainder is the tail design.

## Verdict

Winner: **tail**. Shelve case: **weak** — the tail's empty state *is* the null baseline, and its F1/F2/F5/F8 wins are structural; even the shelve-inclined critique voices conceded it cleanly.

## Grafts onto tail

1. **fold** — the at-caret jot: capture field opens at the caret line (not the footer), Esc commits/returns the caret to the letter; kills eye travel on the near-sacred flow.
2. **spartan** — arrival, not decrement: the parked block visibly departs toward the foot (shipped slide grammar); the chip tick is an event. Fixes the silent-park Spike risk.
3. **spartan** — the one-sentence scope law (surfaces for the audience or the machine end at the seam; the writer's hands never do) to stop tail's per-verb boundary leaks.
4. **verso** — per-region caret/scroll/find restoration, both directions: the skim resumes where it stopped instead of restarting a 3,000-word pile.
5. **margin** — diagnosis-card retirement on park; put back re-arms the next pass.
