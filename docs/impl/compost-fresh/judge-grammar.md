# Judge — grammar lens (coherence with the shipped editor + honest build cost)

Ranking: **tail > spartan > margin > fold > drawer > verso**. Winner: **tail**. Shelve case: **weak**.

## The test I applied

A design is native to Strop if it extends what ships: Esc as go-home (extended, never repurposed, latched where needed), warm/cool/drained speaking once, edges staying with their owners (right margin = annotation lane, bottom = history strip + graveyard chip, tail = the afterlife), boundaries versioned per checkpoint state, and P1–P13 unamended. Between natives, the cheaper build wins. Kill-shots left unanswered count against.

## tail — the plainest native

Blind, this designer re-derived Option B of `docs/impl/07-compost.md` §3: an editable scraps region at the document tail, above the graveyard, seam known to the tool, boundary as a structural node materialized per checkpoint state, footer-chip navigation, Esc home, one downward gradient of aliveness. Both its critique panels said so explicitly ("independent convergence is the strongest evidence yet that the tail is the right place"). F6 is where it wins on grammar: the graveyard already colonized the tail, so story → living scraps → dead record extends the shipped afterlife story instead of forking it — the only design where the writer's existing "down means out of the piece" habit points at the right place. Checkpoint semantics come by the same mechanism 07-compost already commits to (a boundary node per state; nothing teleports).

Every kill-shot has a fix the house prior art already prescribes: Esc-teleport → the R3 excursion latch; one-chip merge → the decided two rhymed chips (B3/N5); provenance chips at rest → asides.md §5's refusal, cured by the practice panel's quiet-provenance graft; missing jot floor → 07-compost's no-travel New scrap + chip pulse; replace-all crossing the seam → align with R5; unspecified Ctrl-Z → spec it. Surgery is subtraction and spec-completion, not redesign. Build: one block-kind, one region predicate consumed by counts/export/AI/cold-read, footer-chip idiom reused, anchor-migration reused, no new surface or focus system. Cheapest real contender.

## spartan — right place, dead soul

Same tail, same gradient, and the best single sentence in the field (the audience/machine vs writer's-hands scope law). But the mechanism — boundary recognized from a magic prose string — dies as critiqued: P1's founding wound (the verb writes a sentence the writer didn't compose), no widget contract (edit a word, the boundary silently dissolves), phrase collision hijacking scope, locale-dependent structure, park feedback as a decrement (the Spike's death). "Kill the string, keep the skeleton" — and the skeleton *is* tail. Second because what survives converges on the winner at near-zero cost.

## margin — a clever native fighting its own house

Reusing the one boundary every scope already respects is the cheapest scope story anywhere. But it contradicts three standing decisions with reasons attached (chirality — asides.md §4; the noun ban — ux-glossary; item chrome on the living pile — asides.md §5), corrupts the lane's anchor grammar (seeds anchored where they struck are about nothing there), and moves unbounded storage into the product's one deliberately rationed surface — the recently-shipped lane budget/recede machinery would be repurposed to evict the writer's darlings. The hoard risk is structural and the critique says it hardens to fatal against a tail-pile rival. It has one.

## fold — an alien mechanic in native clothes

Zero resting chrome and correct colour, but fold-as-atom installs four exceptions to "typing types" at the exact centre of the writing surface (P3/P7): arrow-over, selection atoms, delete-routes-to-graveyard, clipboard flattening. That subverts the manuscript's own grammar — the most sacred contract in the product. Plus the unlatched Esc teleport (R3 re-invented), crease-row chrome, and the practice panel's tense inversion: the parking bet fails, and its mandatory graft sends cuts to a gathered tail. Post-surgery, it too becomes tail.

## drawer — parked option C, reborn unanswered

The house parked the second-column shape for named reasons (two-flow caret surgery; the measure floor at 800 pt); the drawer rebuilds it blind and answers neither. It is the most modal object in the field (focus-scoped find, width-dependent click semantics, overloaded chord, two new Esc meanings), claims a new edge permanently, and splits the afterlives across two navigation idioms — the exact F6 ✗ the extraction audit recorded. Expensive build, and its own best self-criticism (views over one document, not a second container) is simply correct.

## verso — fatal

A full-viewport residence mode at the most mode-dangerous point on a map the product already drew; the dog-ear wears the writer's prose as chrome (P1 verbatim); mirrored lane re-litigates settled chirality; two navigation physics for F6; face-local find re-invents a resolved failure; costly build (second column pipeline, mirrored layout, P6-grade flip). Its own escape hatch is the tail.

## Shelve

Weak. Tail's empty state is pixel-identical to the null baseline, and even the professional shelver conceded the seam + verb + composer core beats failures 1–4 and 6 at near-zero standing chrome. Shelving buys nothing tail's zero-state doesn't already give.

## Grafts onto tail

1. **spartan** — the one-sentence scope law: surfaces speaking for the audience or to the machine end at the seam; the writer's hands never do.
2. **spartan** — arrival-not-decrement: the parked block visibly departs toward the tail via the shipped re-pack slide grammar; the chip pulses as an event (cures tail's silent-tick kill-shot).
3. **drawer** — the count control wears its scope label the moment a second scope exists (`draft · 1,842`; `scraps · 312` while the caret rests there) — clean P12.
4. **margin** — diagnosis-card retirement on park; Put back re-arms the passage for the next pass (machine artifacts never follow writer text out of the machine's scope).
5. **verso** — retrieval lands at the insertion point already *selected*, and every cross-seam move is one undo atom (immediate Ctrl-Z restores verbatim; a just-born seam evaporates).
