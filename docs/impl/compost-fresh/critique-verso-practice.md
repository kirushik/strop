# Critique — design-verso ("The back of the page")

## The Novelist

**The park is right.** One chord, caret pinned, compliance in the corner — the moment the practice is born (research-practice §6.1), and the design spends its budget there. The captions (`cut · 30 Jun · from "…"`) are the provenance that keeps a months-later skim legible with zero filing decision. I would park instead of delete: the whole game, won.

**The jot is broken as specced.** "Opens just under the caret line" plus "the manuscript never reflows" is a contradiction: either the field overlays my next line of prose — P1 forbids overlays on prose, explicitly — or the paragraph reflows, destroying the untouched-screen resumption cue the design itself cites (Altmann & Trafton). Test: caret mid-paragraph, invoke jot, screenshot. Something is occluded or something moved.

**Retrieval, as I actually do it, fails.** Pressfield's flow — shaving, a voice says "the dog and the football," Command-F — is the *only* documented retrieval mechanism, and face-local find breaks its hinge: I know I wrote it; I do not know which side. Find on the front returns nothing, silently. A pile whose contract is "nothing is ever truly lost" (research-practice §2) cannot let its search say *gone* when the answer is *behind you*. The design names this loss and defers it; deferral is wrong — the permission contract itself is leaking.

**The mode error is the month-three killer.** I flip to reread scraps, the phone rings, I return to a warm-ish page with a caret and draft four hundred words of chapter onto the back. Every mitigation is passive: the tone delta dies on a cheap monitor in evening light; the mirrored lane is invisible when empty; the dog-ear is a corner I stopped seeing in week two. The parked past pulses when I type; the back has no active guard, and the failure is the worst kind — manuscript prose silently exiled from export, counts, AI and cold read, discovered at deadline or never.

**Juxtaposition: the design confesses, and the confession is the practice.** "Hunting for the piece that fits" (F3) and the end-of-revision sweep (research-practice §6.7 — the ritual that keeps scrap files alive) both want the pile *adjacent* to the gap. The verso makes every comparison a two-flip round trip with the gap held in memory; the design says an edge drawer does this "better, full stop" — an honest sentence that should worry its author more.

**Emotionally, the shape is true.** Kept-turns vs killed-falls is real; "the back" is a name in the Candy Bag register, and scraps at full measure — prose, not gutter crumbs — respect that parked text is still mine. In month three I'd still park. Whether I'd still *flip* depends on the find graft.

## The Spartan

Rent audit against the null baseline (divider at the bottom, zero UI).

**The boundary the tool knows** — pays rent decisively. Baseline failures 1, 2, 6 cured by construction, not filter. Granted.

**The mode itself** — partially paid. Over a tool-known *region at the tail of the same scroll* (Fountain boneyard, Ulysses' thin line — research-tools §3.1, §3.4), the flip buys full measure for scraps and no horizontal fight at 800 pt. It costs a second caret/scroll/find/selection state, a mirrored margin apparatus (assumption 4 — a whole layout variant purchased to make screenshots distinguishable), a P6-grade turn animation, and the wrong-face error class, which the tail region *cannot have*. And the incumbent's geometry points the other way: every surveyed writer dumps *down* — "piggy bank at the bottom" — and the graveyard already colonized the tail. A living region just above it completes one downward story: *everything out of the piece is below the piece*. The verso teaches a second preposition (behind) no habit points to, then sells the two-preposition system as F6 coherence. F6 "beats" is generous — a well-told story about a fork the design itself introduced.

**The dog-ear** — half rent, one violation. As a resting affordance it is good: visible from day one, already-known form, the F8 answer (P5). But the fold "carrying the first few words of whatever most recently arrived" is the writer's prose worn as chrome — P1's forbid list verbatim, the re-entry-v3 wound itself — and a self-updating text ticker in drafting periphery besides. iA Writer would refuse the ticker and keep the fold.

**The flip animation** — no rent. The design admits crossfade suffices under reduced motion; then crossfade suffices.

**The one-chord grammar** (ctrl-shift-a = "to the other face," arity-sensitive) — pays rent. One verb, own inverse, P8/P13 clean. It survives any shell, including the tail-region shell.

**Scorecard honesty:** F1, F2 (post-surgery), F4, F5 beat. F3 "beats" is overclaimed — it beats the baseline's double scroll but loses to its own confessed alternative on the flow that matters. F6 is a matches dressed as beats. F8 beats *if* the dog-ear reads; the design's own risk list doubts it.

Shelving? No. Boundary-by-construction plus one-chord park is a win the baseline cannot match, and the model under the theatre is nearly minimal. Keep the organ; question the flip.

## Kill-shots

1. **P1 violation, testable by screenshot:** the dog-ear renders the writer's own words as a control's face (latest-scrap preview; parked-caret context). Apply P1's forbid list; it fails as written.
2. **Jot geometry contradiction:** "under the caret line" + "never reflows" ⇒ overlay on prose (P1 forbid) or reflow (cue destroyed). Repro: caret mid-paragraph, 800 pt, invoke jot.
3. **Silent wrong-face drafting:** all cues passive; tone fails under monitor variance, mirrored lane invisible when empty. Repro: flip, idle 20 min, return, type — nothing objects. Cost: prose silently outside export/counts/AI/cold read.
4. **Face-local find breaks the documented retrieval mechanism** (memory-triggered Command-F): a parked phrase searched from the front returns zero results, no signal it exists on the back — "never truly lost" reads as false exactly once, which is enough.
5. **F3 costs two flips per candidate scrap** (gap held in memory); the design concedes an adjacent surface wins "full stop" — and the end-of-revision sweep, the ritual that keeps scrap files alive, is the flow taxed hardest.

## Grafts

1. **Blind corner** — the dog-ear shows only the other face's paper tone (blank vs written); park compliance = the fold thickens momentarily, never quotes words.
2. **Find sees through the paper** — front find's tail carries a quiet count, "2 on the back," navigable; face-local by default, one notch to widen.
3. **The titlebar carries the face** — the editor-state control (already the stance indicator, P12) reads "· the back" while flipped: an active, position-independent guard against kill-shot 3.
4. **Jot in the margin lane** — the capture field opens in the right lane at caret height (established periphery): no overlay, no reflow, same zero-travel return.
5. **The seam fallback** — keep the two-sequence model, verbs, captions and scoping, but render the back as a region past the manuscript's end, above the graveyard: one downward afterlife story, scroll not flip, dog-ear becomes a footer chip. The same design minus the theatre, if the flip fails corridor testing.

## Verdict

**Needs surgery; beats the baseline.** The organ — tool-known boundary, one-chord park, provenance captions, one-verb grammar — is ship-worthy; the shell — text-bearing dog-ear, jot placement, face-local find, unguarded mode — carries two constitutional violations and the month-three decay risk. Grafts 1–4 fit the flip; graft 5 is the escape hatch.
