# Research digest: how writing tools house out-of-manuscript text

Scope: park / jot / retrieve gestures, where scraps live, discovery, complaints — per tool, then lessons. All claims web-verified 2026-07-05; links inline.

## 0 · The folk practice itself

[Reproof surveyed writers](https://www.reproof.app/blog/writing-and-the-cutting-room-floor) on what they do with cuts: the dominant pattern is **dump at the bottom of the current document** ("I dump them at the bottom of the document until I'm sure the piece is done"; a "piggy bank"; a "Things cut" section in Google Docs). Others start a separate outtakes file; some delete and trust version history. Reuse is real but asymmetric: freelancers report cut sections resurfacing in *later, different* pieces more often than back into the same one. Writing-craft blogs confirm the named habit ("darling graveyard", Mike Schur's "candy jar"): parking works because it *feels* like moving, not killing ([Reedsy](https://reedsy.com/blog/kill-your-darlings/)). The brief's null baseline is not hypothetical — it is the incumbent.

## 1 · Tool by tool

**Scrivener** — Park: no verb; users cut, navigate the binder, paste into a self-made "Cut"/"Outtakes" folder — [officially recommended workflow](https://www.literatureandlatte.com/blog/how-to-clean-up-a-messy-scrivener-binder), works only because non-Draft folders are excluded from compile and targets. Jot: per-document Inspector notes, or the **Scratchpad** (Cmd-Shift-Return): a floating window whose notes live *outside every project* in a folder of RTFs ([L&L blog](https://www.literatureandlatte.com/blog/how-to-use-the-scrivener-scratchpad-to-collect-research-and-capture-ideas)) — "send to project" exists, but scraps don't travel with the document. Retrieve: drag from binder back into Draft. Discovery: poor — blog posts titled "[Lost Your Scratchpad?](https://phsolomon.com/2017/10/31/lost-your-scratchpad-here-it-is-in-scrivener/)" exist; **Snapshots** (per-document frozen copies) are chronically [misunderstood and underused](https://forum.literatureandlatte.com/t/problem-understanding-snapshots/17691) (users expect project-level). Complaint pattern: four overlapping afterlives (Trash, cut folder, snapshots, Scratchpad), none primary; Trash-as-storage is [warned against](https://www.literatureandlatte.com/blog/understanding-the-scrivener-trash-folder).

**Ulysses** — Park/Jot: **material sheets** ([Ulysses 19](https://blog.ulysses.app/material-sheets/)): any sheet toggles regular↔material (Ctrl-Cmd-M); material sheets are **excluded from statistics, export, and goals** while staying fully editable, marked only by a **thin red line** in the sheet list. Also older per-sheet attached notes in the dashboard. Retrieve: toggle back, or copy text across; sheet-level granularity means you can't park a *paragraph* without making a new sheet. Where: same group as the manuscript, interleaved. Discovery: buried in File menu / swipe menu; reviewers liked it, [MacStories](https://www.macstories.net/reviews/ulysses-19-brings-cursor-support-external-folders-material-sheets-and-more/) framed it as "research next to text". This is the cleanest existing "same place, different scope" design.

**Highland 2** — ships the split explicitly: the **Bin** (sidebar shelf; drag selected text out of the script into it — the drag cuts; Alt-drag copies; Edit-menu "Cut/Copy to Bin" as fallback; drag back in to retrieve) and the **Scratchpad** (a free-text notes tab "outside the document") ([Bela Lampert how-to](https://www.belalampert.com/2022/01/use-highland-2s-bin-to-copy-and-move-blocks-of-text-in-your-screenplay-document/), [ScreenCraft](https://screencraft.org/blog/top-10-features-youll-love-in-highland-2-5-updated-screenwriting-software-from-screenwriter-john-august/)). Bin blocks edit only through a double-click popup editor — scraps become card-widgets, not text. Praised against Final Draft ([review](https://robertbrucecarter.com/writing/2018/12/a-review-of-highland-2-vs-final-draft/)); grumbles: the drag needs a held click before moving, and the Bin is per-document with no search.

**Final Draft** — Jot: **ScriptNotes** (Cmd-Shift-K), colored markers beside the page, viewable in a panel, excluded from print/PDF ([FD KB](https://kb.finaldraft.com/hc/en-us/articles/27716323130644-How-do-I-insert-notes-into-my-script-ScriptNotes)). Park: **Alt Dialogue** — unlimited alternate takes attached *in place* to a speech; only the displayed alt prints ([FD blog](https://www.finaldraft.com/blog/give-your-characters-options-the-power-of-alternate-dialogue)). No general cut-shelf: professionals keep scenes in a second file or scroll-to-bottom. Alt Dialogue is the strongest "retrieval = swap in place, anchored at origin" precedent.

**Fountain** (plain-text screenplay markup) — the **boneyard**: `/* … */` comments out any span, unlimited length, excluded from print/export; `[[notes]]` for inline jots; both are *convention the format knows* ([fountain.io/syntax](https://fountain.io/syntax/)). Park = wrap in `/* */` where it stands: zero travel, boundary survives export/counts. Complaints: syntax must be learned; interop is lossy (apps [drop or mangle notes/boneyards on import](https://forum.literatureandlatte.com/t/fountain-screenplay-import-notes-and-boneyards/50209)); parked text stays visually in your way mid-scene.

**iA Writer** — the instructive refusal: no bin, no snapshots, no notes panel; "fewer features, by design" ([ia.net/writer](https://ia.net/writer)). Its answers are the Library with hashtags (a scrap is just another file, organized by [writing tags into the text](https://ia.net/writer/blog/write-to-organize)) and **Content Blocks** — `/file.md` on its own line transcludes another file into export ([docs](https://ia.net/writer/support/library/content-blocks)) — i.e. manuscript assembled *from* scraps rather than scraps extracted from manuscript. iA bets the file system plus convention beats an in-app home.

**Org-mode** — the deepest grammar: **drawers** (`:NAME: … :END:`, folded to one line in place, [manual](https://orgmode.org/manual/Drawers.html)); **COMMENT** keyword on a headline excludes the whole subtree from export ([manual](https://orgmode.org/manual/Comment-Lines.html)); **org-capture** (global chord opens a micro-buffer, `C-c C-c` files the note to a preset target and *restores your window configuration exactly* — the canonical zero-residue jot, [guide](https://orgmode.org/guide/Capture-Refile-Archive.html)); **refile** (`C-c C-w`) moves a subtree without visiting the destination; **archive** moves finished trees to a sibling file. Complaint: power via memorized chords; nothing is discoverable by sight.

**Obsidian** — no native home; the community fills it with "extract note" plugins (Note Composer core plugin: selection → new note, replaced by a link) ([forum](https://forum.obsidian.md/t/extract-note-refactor/733)). Complaint gold: users repeatedly ask for extraction **without the residue link** ([forum](https://forum.obsidian.md/t/extract-to-new-note-and-keep-original-text/31430)) — parking that leaves a visible stump in the prose is felt as damage.

**Notion** — folk patterns only: toggle blocks as in-page hiding, Archive pages/status. Writers report the database-first structure forces structure-before-writing and leave for Obsidian ([XDA](https://www.xda-developers.com/obsidian-vs-notion-writing/)); archives require pre-built systems.

**novelWriter** — notes are first-class *documents outside the manuscript tree*; `%comment` and `%synopsis:` lines inside manuscript docs are excluded from builds; the manuscript build step explicitly filters what's included ([docs](https://novelwriter.io/docs/usage/comments.html)). Same shape as Scrivener's Draft/non-Draft split, but the boundary is markup, not folders.

**Scapple / Tinderbox** — pre-manuscript surfaces: freeform note canvases, drag notes into Scrivener's binder when ready ([Scapple](https://www.literatureandlatte.com/scapple/overview)). Precedent for *seeds living on a different surface than prose* — and for the cost: a second app/window nobody opens mid-sentence.

**Self-found precedents.** (1) **Microsoft Word's Spike**: Ctrl-F3 cuts the selection onto a named accumulator, no travel, paste all with Ctrl-Shift-F3 — in Word since 97 and [famously unknown](https://www.howtogeek.com/213212/how-to-use-the-spike-to-copy-and-paste-text-in-word-2013/): the perfect park gesture killed by invisibility (no feedback, no resting place on screen). (2) **Drafts** ("[Where Text Starts](https://getdrafts.com/)"): capture-first inbox — type now, file later; proves jot cost can be near zero when the destination decision is deferred. (3) **Gingko**: card-tree writing where fragments simply sit as sibling cards until promoted ([gingkowriter.com](https://gingkowriter.com/)) — no park verb at all because nothing is "in" the manuscript until compiled.

## 2 · Cross-cutting lessons, ranked

1. **Scope exclusion is the entire value.** Every success (Ulysses material sheets, Fountain boneyard, org COMMENT, novelWriter, Scrivener non-Draft) wins by making the tool *know* the boundary so export/counts/review respect it. Chrome without scope-knowledge loses to the divider convention.
2. **Two organs recur: a cut-shelf and a jot-pad.** Tools that ship both, distinctly (Highland Bin + Scratchpad; FD Alts + ScriptNotes), read clean; tools with four overlapping afterlives (Scrivener) breed confusion about where anything went.
3. **Park must be no-travel AND visibly compliant.** Spike: no travel, invisible → unused for 25+ years. Scrivener: visible but travel-expensive. Highland's drag: both, and it's the loved one — but fiddly mechanics tax it.
4. **Zero-residue matters in both directions.** Obsidian users revolt against stump links; org-capture's restore-window-config is the gold standard for the jot round trip.
5. **Scraps must stay text and stay in the document's file.** Highland's popup-editor cards and Scrivener's external Scratchpad folder both violate expectations (editability; portability).
6. **Discovery dies in menus, lives in the selection path.** Every underused feature here (Spike, Scratchpad, Snapshots) is chord- or menu-only; every used one sits where the hand already is (drag target, sidebar always faintly present, red line in the list you already scan).
7. **Retrieval is rare; anchoring it at origin (FD Alts, graveyard-style "put back") beats a browse-and-paste library.**

## 3 · Design implications for F1–F8, ranked

1. **F5 first, and loudly**: the boundary the tool knows — excluded from export/counts/AI, with a quiet always-visible marker at the boundary (Ulysses' thin red line is the minimal precedent). This alone beats the null baseline's failures 1–2, 6.
2. **F1**: park = one chord from the selection flank, destination visible from where you sit (Highland's legibility, Spike's economy, none of either's tax). Compliance shown at the *destination edge*, not by a dialog.
3. **F2**: jot = org-capture shape — a transient input that commits and returns the caret exactly; never a panel to visit (baseline failure 4).
4. **F8**: formalize the convention writers already invented — a region under a divider, in-document, in-file, editable text (P3). The invitation is the divider being *already there* to type under, not a tour; Drafts proves defer-the-filing lowers the start cost.
5. **F7**: serve all three tenses in one editable region rather than three homes — Scrivener's four-afterlife confusion is the cautionary tale; Highland justifies at most a two-way split (living scraps vs record), which Strop already has via the graveyard.
6. **F6**: deliberate-vs-automatic must differ in *state* (editable warm vs read-only drained), not in *place-logic* — one spatial story, like Draft-folder-vs-Trash but adjacent.
7. **F3**: round trip = Esc-restores-position, org-capture style; no residue (lesson 4).
8. **F4**: retrieval as "put back / swap in place" anchored at origin (FD Alts, graveyard parity) rather than a library-browse; weight it below parking — evidence says reuse mostly happens in *other* pieces, so v1 retrieval can be plain text-move.
