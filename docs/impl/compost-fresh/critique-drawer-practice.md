# Critique — design-drawer.md ("Scraps"), practice panel

Voices: the Novelist (mid-book, keeps a messy cuts file) and the Spartan (default vote: shelve). Sources: the brief, research-practice.md, research-tools.md, research-theory.md, design-principles.md.

## Where both voices concede first

The core beats the null baseline, and not narrowly: a third sibling text region the tool *knows*, serialized per checkpoint, dissolves baseline failures 1, 2 and 6 by construction rather than by patch. And `Set aside` resting on the selection menu between Annotate and Exile is F8 executed exactly as the practice digest prescribes — the affordance at the hesitating hand, structural, never promotional. The name register (Scraps / Set aside / Take back / Put back) is the product's voice. The fight is not over the region or the verb. It is over the drawer as their body.

## The Novelist

**The jot I would actually make has no door.** F2 is where I live — the stray line arriving mid-sentence — and the design's only entrance to it is a memorized chord with no selection. No selection means no menu; no menu means the F8 invitation, which the design correctly builds for parking, never fires for jotting. The tools digest is unambiguous: every chord-only capture organ in the survey died unused — the Spike sat in Word for twenty-five years, "famously unknown." Even after I learn `Set aside` on selections, nothing tells me the same chord with nothing selected does something else. The design's most evidence-critical flow (theory digest: "the feature is won or lost here") is its least discoverable.

**And when I do find it, Enter betrays me.** The capture line commits on Enter. I am a prose writer mid-burst; Enter is a paragraph break in every text I own (P8: one gesture, one meaning). My two-sentence thought gets chopped at the first Enter and shipped behind the spine half-finished; continuing it now costs open-drawer, find entry, click, resume — the full round trip the capture line existed to avoid.

**The stale-selection ambush.** One chord, two verbs, switched by selection state. I select a paragraph, scroll away to check something, decide to jot — chord. No capture line appears (the paragraph parks silently, off-screen); I start typing my jot and it lands in the manuscript where my selection just was. A Raskin mode error the design's own theory digest warns against, with manuscript text displaced as the cost.

**Month three.** My bottom-of-document pile survives because I scroll past it on the way to the end — the accidental re-read ritual. The practice digest says that ritual is what separates living files from dead ones (§5c: the lawyer's end-of-draft read-back, Johnson's quarterly re-read), and that piles demanding nothing but receiving no glance become the collector's stack, "ignored entirely." The drawer removes the baseline's ritual and attaches none: nothing in any workflow ever carries me past my scraps. Parking will feel wonderful — the warm sheet genuinely reads as moving, not killing, so F1's permission function is served — but the count will tick up behind a closed edge I stop seeing. Emotionally right at cut-time, decaying by month three.

**Where I'd look.** Twenty years of habit puts cut text at the bottom. Hunting in March for the paragraph I set aside in January, I will scroll to the tail, find only the drained graveyard, and have a small heart attack before remembering the edge. The design calls F6 a draw; from inside the habit it reads worse — beside-vs-below is a told story, and the brief's baseline failure 5 (two afterlives, unrelated places, unrelated habits) survives in softened form.

## The Spartan

**Rent audit.** Delete the drawer's rendering — spine, slide layer, overlay/dock fork, yielding law, hollow caret, focus-scoped find — and keep the third region rendered in-flow at the document's tail, above the graveyard, with the same `Set aside` verb doing the travel so the caret never moves. What survives: F5 entirely (the region is the scope win), F1 (verb, zero caret travel, compliance at the footer chip), F2's capture-if-fixed, F4, F7, F8, and F6 *improved* — parked and dead text adjacent in one scroll, the spatial story shown instead of told, and the Novelist's ritual restored for free. What dies: side-by-side compare at 1600pt. That is the entire exclusive rent of a permanent edge, four new interaction laws, and — by the design's own §6 confession — "the most modal object in the product." The design's named loss #1 quotes the Engelbart digest against itself: the missing half is views over one document, not a second container. When a design's best self-criticism is simply correct, believe it. iA Writer would refuse this drawer and ship the region.

**Mode ledger, itemized.** Click into prose: closes the drawer at 800pt, doesn't at 1600 — a habit built on the laptop breaks on the desktop. Ctrl-F: corpus depends on focus, but Pressfield's retrieval fires memory-first ("Command-F finds it") with no scope check; half those searches hit the wrong corpus and report a lying zero softened only by a tail row. Enter: paragraphs everywhere, ships in the capture line. Ctrl-shift-a: two verbs, one chord. Raskin is cited in the spec and contradicted four times.

**Geometry at 800pt.** The capture line "slides out of the left edge at the caret's height." At 800pt the gutter is a sliver, so caret height means the line under composition: the capture line lands on the prose being written — P1 forbids chrome on top of prose, and the drawer's own yielding law concedes the point everywhere except here. The overlay also covers line *beginnings*, the worst side to occlude for reading.

## Kill-shots

1. Jot is chord-only — no selection, no menu, no invitation thread; the Spike precedent predicts near-zero discovery. Test: corridor stranger asked to "note a stray thought without leaving the sentence" finds no affordance.
2. Overloaded ctrl-shift-a: a stale or off-screen selection turns a jot into a silent park, then the jot text types into the manuscript. Test: select, scroll away, chord.
3. Enter commits the capture line — mid-thought Enter (paragraph habit, P8) chops and ships the jot; resuming costs the full trip the line was built to avoid.
4. No re-read ritual attached to any workflow moment; the baseline's scroll-past ritual is removed and unreplaced. Prediction: park-rate rises, open-rate collapses by month three (instrumentable).
5. The drawer chrome fails the rent test: an in-flow tail region plus the same verb keeps every scorecard win except wide-window side-by-side, at zero new modes; the spine, yielding law, focus-scoped find, and 800/1600 click divergence are overhead. At 800pt the capture line and overlay sit on prose (P1).

## Grafts

1. The severity gradient on the selection menu — Annotate / Set aside / Exile — is the best F8 mechanism in the batch; graft it onto any winner.
2. Scrapyard as a third sibling text region serialized per checkpoint state (boundary versioning by construction); the load-bearing architecture, independent of the drawer.
3. Scope-announcing count labels on the control itself (`draft · 1,842 words` / `scraps · 312`) — clean P12.
4. Find's cross-surface tail row (`3 in scraps`, click crosses over) — keep the row, drop the focus-scoped mode: find defaults to the manuscript everywhere.
5. Fix the jot: give it a visible resting place (the region's ready caret slot, or an omnibar `set aside…` row), let Enter grow the line, commit only on Esc/click-away.

## Verdict

Needs surgery. The region and the verb beat the baseline decisively; the drawer body should be re-housed in-flow beside the graveyard, the jot given a door and a safe Enter, and the mode stack (find scope, width-dependent click, overloaded chord) dismantled.
